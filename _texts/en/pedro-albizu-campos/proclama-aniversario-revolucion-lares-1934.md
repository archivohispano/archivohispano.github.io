The Yankee disposes. In reprisal against the Yankee tariffs all
nations close access to their markets to us.
The remedy is unique, and it is the independence of Puerto Rico.
It is already clear to everyone that the economy is a content of
politics.
Only the government of the Republic will be able to resolve all its problems for the good
of the Homeland.
On September 23, 1868, the heroes of Lares
taught us that history is written with the noblest blood of the
nationality, and that independence, which is the supreme good of a
people, is conquered only with courage and sacrifice.
Nationalism goes to Lares to invoke the memory of the patriotic manes
to fulfill its duty to constitute the free Republic,
sovereign and independent of Puerto Rico.
Given in San Juan de Puerto Rico on the 16th day of September
1934, sixty-sixth year of the Proclamation of the Republic.
Nationalist Party of Puerto Rico
The National Board
P. ALBIZU CAMPOS
President

Lares, Puerto Rico
September 23
,
By Juan Antonio Corretjer
"Forward, March!" The Cadets entering Lares in the
morning. The "Suárez Díaz" battalion of Utuado opens the column and
the powerful Caguas battalion closes it, rifle on shoulder. Precious girls, in whose gaze shines the sun of an intact innocence,
carry, unfurled, a beautiful national flag. The "Bracetti" battalion renders
military honors. and behind, the pilgrim multitude, which from the smaller islands to the virginal mountains comes
to Lares to ratify before the tomb of the immortals its loyalty to the
Homeland and its devotion to Liberty.

At the conclusion, homage is paid before the obelisk in the plaza of the
Revolution.
Afternoon Events:
1-Military Competition.
At 3:00 P.M. clarions and drums announce the beginning of
the exercises. Five towns compete in the closed column exercises competition. The trophy goes to the "Suárez Díaz," of
Utuado. The decision was unanimous.

2-Presentation of the Flag of the Race.
The battalions form a rectangle around which a multitude huddles,
larger as the day advances. Silence is played. Within the rectangle, Mrs. Trina Padilla Vda. de Sanz,
Mrs. Amparo Díaz de Suárez, Mrs. Josefina Cuebas, Mrs.
Herminia Méndez and Mr. President. Three bare flagpoles
point to the sky. In the silence of the afternoon, the voice of Mr. President
announces that, at that moment, the ceremony will be carried out to
raise the Flag of the Race, representative of civilization in the
world, conceived and designed by the illustrious Captain Don Ángel
Camblor, of the Army of Uruguay, Plenipotentiary Delegate of
Puerto Rican Nationalism to Uruguay. The instructions
for the design were sent to nationalism by the Commission
of the Flag of the Race attached to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs
of the Uruguayan government.
Now to raise, on the left, the flag of Lares. Mrs. Herminia Méndez, granddaughter of Don Aurelio Méndez,
hero of the day of Lares and member of the provisional government
of '68, holds the flagpole. Mrs. Josefina Cuebas, niece of the patriotic
heroine, Marina Bracetti, raises the flag -arm
of gold that embroidered the flag in the
dawn of our freedom and spirit of steel that resisted all the
tests at the zenith of our heroism. Lieutenant Don Francisco Sabat Alvarez, of the "Suárez Díaz" makes a guard of honor. The flag rises.
Now to raise the mono-starred flag, the National Pavilion
-the shroud of Pachín Marín- the pavilion that, according to Pagán
Rodríguez, "Suárez Díaz brandished to enter the heavens." Lieutenant Don Adrián Cortés, of the "Suárez Díaz," holds the pole, and the hero's mother, Mrs. Amparo Diaz de Suárez, raises the flag.
In the center, Mrs. Trina Padilla Vda. de Sanz, the illustrious daughter of
El Caribe. Captain Don Efraim Pérez, of the "Suárez
Díaz," holds the pole. The Daughter of El Caribe raises the flag of the Race. Immediately
, she reads some beautiful allusive verses.
3-War Exercises.
Before the pavilions that the south breeze beats in joy, the battalion of Caguas unfolds. The Adjutant General Don Antonio Rodríguez commands. The exhibition consists of three movements: an
attack, a bayonet counterattack, and a change of positions
under fire. Before the almost unexpected skill, accuracy, and discipline
the people break into cheers and applause.
4-National Assembly.
In the National Assembly, Mr. President Albizu Campos opens with the words of ritual; Mr. Secretary General, Don José Lameiro, reads the Proclamation of the day; and the Delegate for Arecibo to the National Board who subscribes to this review acts as Introducer.
The youth, represented by Mr. President of the National Federation of Puerto Rican Students, member of the Ibero-American Federation of Students, Don Francisco Pagán Rodríguez, render verbal homage to the heroes; the procerity, represented by the venerable Don Julio Medina González; the woman, represented by the poetess Mrs. Trina Padilla Vda. de Sanz; and the Presidency of the Nationalist Party in person.
Presidential Speech.
We will try to inform the ideology expressed in the presidential speech
that closed the assembly.
Two Experiences and One Pain:
"You have heard the speeches of Don Julio Medina González
and Mrs. Trina Padilla. In them you have verified the presence
of two great and distinct experiences in the heat of a great, unique
pain. Great, immense is the pain with which the full intellective consciousness wounds the presence of slavery, aggravated in its natural
anguish by being that of our brothers. And great is the tenacity,
great is the will that must inform that consciousness to maintain itself on an unbreakable straight path. Patriotism is will and
effort. Not sacrifice. For the patriot, duty is not sacrifice. Duty is life."
All or Nothing:
"Patriotism requires the offering. And in this there is no partiality.
Either all or nothing. We can, in the hallucination of self-love
believe we have done much. We have done nothing. Because
patriotism is not self-love but patriotic love."
"Life must be crowned with a supreme act. Faced with any
weakness, you must start again. No one advances with a guilty
conscience. Conscience is rudder and is sail and demands purity by
hand and by wind."
