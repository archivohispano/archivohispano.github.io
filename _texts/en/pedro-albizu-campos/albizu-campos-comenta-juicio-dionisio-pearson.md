Letter to the National Federation of Students

Aguas Buenas de Puerto Rico,
March 15, 1936
To don Joaquín Rodríguez Benítez,
President of the National Federation of
Puerto Rican Students:
To don José R. Lebrón Velázquez,
President of the National Federation of Students
of the Superior School;
To all the Noble and Valiant Student Body
of Puerto Rico:
The valiant and patriotic youth constitutes the sacred
posterity of the Homeland.
It is the reserve that must be cared for as the source of our
eternal nationality.
It is the duty of adults to confront with valor and sacrifice
all the problems of the Homeland, especially when it is expected
to offer life to make the existence and the Independence of the Nation respected. In these precise moments of our
history, your parents are fulfilling their duty. Valiant and
self-denying they serve as shields to their children against the excesses
of a despicable empire.
The youth must not abandon their classrooms. In their
privileged forum they must wait serenely and alert for the moment of
the fulfillment of their duties to the Homeland.
Your organization instills terror in foreign despotism.
Keep it prepared and disciplined for the
decisive hour.

El Mundo, March 16, 1936, p. 1 and 4.

With the respect and emotion that inspire your solid
patriotic solidarity, I allow myself to advise you not to go on
any strike.
In the classrooms your power is omnipotent.
The most powerful and brutal of empires cannot violate
without grave consequences for its security, the sacred forum of the
student body.
Trust in the adults: in your parents and in your
older brothers. They will fulfill their duty in any
situation that Yankee imperialism may present to us.
Do not rush into the fight. In the national moral
restoration, the adults cannot decline to the student body the
fulfillment of any patriotic duty.
Remain serene and alert.
May your motto be: Valor and Sacrifice.

P. ALBIZU CAMPOS.

Albizu Campos comments on the
trial against
Dionisio Pearson

In yesterday's session, the Prosecutor proposed to the defense that
they accept the testimony of Mr. Francisco Navarro, Jr.,
in its entirety, in order to close his case. The defense agreed to the
Prosecutor's request on the condition that the Prosecutor present
the testimony of policeman Bonilla and of Messrs. Betancourt and
Sulsona, very important testimonies that he had presented
the Prosecutor in the case, before dissolving the first Jury. The Prosecutor
then announced that he had discarded Messrs. Bonilla, Betancourt, and Sulsona as his witnesses. The defense then
announced that it could not agree to the Prosecutor's request
with respect to the testimony of Francisco Navarro, Jr., because if
this testimony suited the Prosecutor's theory, it suited the
interest of public justice that the Prosecutor re-present
his witnesses, Messrs. Bonilla, Betancourt, and Sulsona.

If said testimony suited the Prosecutor's theory, he should
accept the rest of his own evidence presented by him, and thus the
Jury could reach a fair verdict.

The Prosecutor has remained firm in discarding
Messrs. Bonilla, Betancourt, and Sulsona, testimonies that prove
conclusively that don José Santiago, was brutal-
ly murdered by the Police at the entrance of the University
on October 24 of last year.

El Mundo, March 16, 1936, p. 8. Dionisio Pearson, the only survivor of the
Masacre de Río Piedras that occurred on October 24, 1935, was accused of the crimes
of murder, attempted murder, and carrying weapons. He was put on trial, and Albizu
Campos acted as his principal defense lawyer, along with other prestigious
lawyers. He was acquitted by the jury on March 18, 1936.
