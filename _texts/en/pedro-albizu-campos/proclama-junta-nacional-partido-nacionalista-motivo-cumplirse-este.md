A PROCLAMATION OF THE NATIONAL BOARD OF THE
NATIONALIST PARTY
ON THE OCCASION OF THE 63RD
ANNIVERSARY OF THE REBELLION OF LARES

"The Revolution to establish the Independence of the Homeland has never
failed. Nationality has been defined. The sacrifice of its
martyrs consecrated its sovereignty. On that glorious date the will
to be free was fixed, and from it begins the chronology of the
Independent Republic, no matter how long it takes to free its sovereignty from
foreign interventions.

"September 23 is the supreme day of the Homeland's feat. Sixty-
three years will have passed since the patriots of Lares proclaimed
the Republic of Puerto Rico.

"The uprising imposed the series of reforms that culminated in the
Autonomic Charter of 1897. A people defined by arms is a respectable
and indestructible people.

"The baptism of sacrifice is national and encompasses posterity
so that, enlightened by a distinguished example, it may consecrate itself to the decisive effort
of absolute liberation.

("If the ties of blood, religion, culture, and the community
of interests were not obstacles to the Independence of the Mother
Homeland Spain, when there is no bond that links us to the
imperial power of the United States, which, currently, in violation of all
rights, makes it impossible for us by force to exercise our powers as a
sovereign nation, if not, on the contrary, everything separates us from these invaders
and our vital interests are in serious conflict with theirs, to our detriment, it is imperative to declare roundly that the
immediate Constitution of the Republic is unpostponable.

"The Municipal Assembly of Lares has ordered that September
23 be celebrated as a holiday. This is a consecration and an example
to follow. San Sebastián will respond in the same way because if in Lares
the insurrection began, in el Pepino the main combat of the
distinguished day was fought. We urge the other municipal governments of the country,
which are the legitimate representation of popular sovereignty, to
adhere to the patriotic and just resolution of the government of Lares and
decree a holiday in their respective jurisdictions the
glorious anniversary that is the purest tribute of national decorum. Compatriots

LETTER TO MR. LUIS VENEGAS CORTES
San Juan, P.R., September 19, 1930
Mr. Luis Venegas Cortés,
President of the University Committee
Pro-Victims of Santo Domingo,
Río Piedras, P.R.

Friend of all my esteem:

I acknowledge receipt of your moving communication accompanied by
check number 7402, drawn by the University, charged to the "Banco
Territorial y Agrícola" of Río Piedras and endorsed to me, for the
sum of four hundred ninety-eight dollars and fifty cents,
amount of the funds raised to date among university students
to help the victims of Santo Domingo.
You inform me that the Dominican University Committee Pro-Victims
agreed to make the remittance through the mediation of the Party
Nationalist.

On behalf of our Party I accept the noble commission and
I will immediately send to the Most Excellent Mr. Rafael Leonidas Trujillo,
President of the Dominican Republic, the funds placed at the
disposal of his government for the charitable purpose you have
arranged.

No Puerto Rican act in the face of the catastrophe that has overwhelmed us
with sorrow has the significance of this yours. It indicates, first, the
clear orientation of university students in national and international
issues. In that heroic nation, noble and
hospitable, to the point of exaggeration, if one can exaggerate in that order,
no memory of its immense sorrow will produce the emotion that
yours will. Here is an absolute selflessness and a love of brothers to
brothers.

The ideal of the Antillean Confederation will be realized by the present
Antillean youth because they are willing to create a power that must
impose itself on every invader.

This heartfelt act of yours is the message of solidarity that announces the
era that is already glimpsed.

Our Party, which in reality is not a Party, but the Homeland
organized to rescue its sovereignty, so that even those who today fight
Independence may enjoy the dignity of being free, enjoys the respect and sympathy
of all Dominicans because of the purpose it pursues. And it will be pleasing to them that this demonstration of
your sensitivity arrives through our mediation.

With my cordial congratulations for the work of nobles,

I am yours affectionately,

P. ALBIZU CAMPOS

ALBIZU CAMPOS SPEAKS

Since the crowd did not fit in the theater, the public asked that Mr.
Albizu Campos speak on the tribune of the plaza since the threats
of rain had passed.
When the President of the Nationalist Party climbed the tribune he was
received with a prolonged burst of applause.
"Today a supreme desire of my life is fulfilled in finding myself in
this city of Lares. I would have entered Lares on my knees: one enters
holy land on one's knees. The men and women of the generation
current do not even know how to walk and get tired walking two kilometers
even if it is to pay homage to the heroes of our freedom. Our
grandparents did not know the automobile, nor the electric train and yet,
they performed wonders. They did not know the paved roads, nor the
modern means of communication, but they had a physical and
moral strength superior to us, and they climbed those peaks with ease and risked their lives for an ideal. What consisted of the superiority of
those heroes? In that they were men of high principles in their
conscience. Devotion to an idea is not enough, consecration
to the idea is necessary and this implies sacrifice.
"You are not like the hero Ramírez because you do not have the
consciousness of consecration to an ideal, nor the consciousness of your
own strength. The day that happens the independence of Puerto Rico will
have been achieved as a fatality in history.
"The force that weighs on our people is the sense of
impotence. We must evoke, then, the contrary forces, the forces of
wisdom and beauty immanent in the nature of man.

a microphone installed on this tribune I can inform the world of
what is happening in Puerto Rico.

THE NATIONALIST ACT OF SUNDAY IN PONCE
THE SPEECH DELIVERED BY LCDO.
P. ALBIZU CAMPOS
When Albizu Campos climbed the tribune, the people of Ponce
received him with a prolonged ovation. The President of the
Nationalist Party delivered an eloquent and substantial speech that
extended for two and a half hours.
"My beloved people: I want to greet you after three years of
absence through our America. A deep emotion overwhelms my
spirit in presenting myself before the people of Ponce who saw me born. To love
the land that gave us life is the first duty of man. I want to send
a collective greeting from this tribune to my school classmates, to
my physical and moral protectors, to my friends and fellow townsmen.
"Tonight we celebrate the great epic of the race that discovered
and civilized this continent. To the Mother Spain we owe the immense
gratitude for this work without parallels in human history. With the feat
of Columbus the spherical shape of the planet was definitively proven.
With this discovery man realized that he did not live on an
isolated land but that he belonged to a celestial system of which he was its
collaborator and interpreter. Man was then aware of the
infinity of the universe and that within himself were enclosed the possibilities of
infinite progress. His concept of the universe and of his
creator was amplified, and he understood that his mission was to interpret the laws that governed it
and live the higher life of the spirit".
After a philosophical digression on the meaning of the
discovery and its transcendence in the life of Western culture
he went on to analyze the transcendental orientation of nationalism
"Nationalism is the force that rises against any power
that denies us personality. It is a movement that aspires to
awaken the forces of wisdom in the people, to save the
nationality for culture and history. It wants this people who are
the first spiritual unity to crystallize on the continent, which is the
most culturally and racially integrated nationality in America, which is
an improvable and sensitive mass, not to be annulled and to be reintegrated into the life
of culture through the full affirmation of its personality. The
nationalism is not merely the reintegration of lands into Puerto Rican hands, nor the salvation of its commerce and its finances: it is the
nationality standing to rescue its sovereignty and save this people
for the higher values of life. Colonialism is the annulment and the
absorption of our moral forces; independence is spiritual fullness, it is enjoyment of all the physical and moral gifts that God
deposited in our land. If the law denies personality to a madman

it also denies him his capacity to verify any legal transaction.
If a people is denied its collective personality, it is denied the
capacity to govern its own destiny and we are placed on the level of an
irresponsible madman. Nationalism is not a party in the limiting sense
of the word but a movement that tends to save all the
constitutive values of nationality. That is why we will not rest until
the flag of the United States has been lowered from Puerto Rico".
The speaker then analyzed the historical, sociological and
cultural panorama of the Ibero-American nations and stressed the need to
maintain intense ties with brother peoples "because the
case of Puerto Rico is the evident example of the intentions that
United States has for Latin America"
"America is the richest continent in the world due to its
territorial extension, its population and its immense natural resources. That is why it is
the object of the world's greed. It is becoming aware of this
phenomenon and remains on guard against exotic aggressions. She
knows the greedy intentions of the United States and that is why
all the governments that the people suspect are subjected
to the Yankees are falling".
He then commented on the policy of the United States in the Caribbean. "The
Caribbean Sea is the heart of the world. From here depart and converge the
maritime currents of the tropics that carry heat and life to all the
continents of the earth. This will be the theater of the great war that is eight
or ten years away to overthrow the imperialist power of the
United States. We must prepare for that hour which will be that of our
political salvation".
He then made an analysis of the problems of the United States, of the
North American social life, of its attitude towards the black race, etc.
He announced that one of the most
bloody social revolutions in history awaited him, since there "the exploitation exercised against the
people had made the communist seed prosper in a
dangerous way for the stability of its plutocratic institutions".
He commented on the recent speeches of President Hoover in which he
recognizes the importance that these movements have taken when he
takes the trouble to comment on them. He also censured the society of
Ponce for the welcome it gave to the American sailors. "The slave
can never pay homage to the master," he said.
At the end of his speech Albizu Campos, the public burst into
a delirious applause carrying him on their shoulders throughout the plaza while
the band played the melodious chords of la Borinqueña.
