LETTER TO MR. LUIS SANCHEZ MORALES72

July 1, 1932.

Hon. Luis Sánchez Morales,
President of the Senate and to the
Gentlemen Senators.

Gentlemen:
In our memorial filed before the legislature at the opening of
the present extraordinary session, by virtue of a mandate of the National
Board of the Nationalist party of Puerto Rico, we demand for
our patriotic organization equality before the electoral law. The Prensa
of today informs us that that body has approved in third reading the
draft Electoral Law submitted to its consideration. To
Nationalism was not granted the opportunity to be heard despite
the terms of his already cited memorial. The project has been approved
unanimously although it does not take into consideration the situation of the
Nationalist party.
This action of the Senate is simultaneous with the letter from the Executive
Secretary communicating to us the suspension of the registration of the new
candidacies that we have filed in his secretariat, alleging the expectation
of a new opinion from the attorney general that illustrates him about the
right to register candidacies by convention, when by right already
recognized, the Nationalist party has filed general
candidacies.
We see in all that machinations of the intervening governor to
prevent Nationalism from going to the polls. What happened in the year 1924 is repeated. It is hard for us to believe that that body without
distinction of parties has entered into an anti-patriotic intelligence
with the North American power, to prevent the nationalist
will of the country from manifesting itself peacefully in the electoral contests.
Given the gravity of the situation that such a policy could bring if it is proven to be true, it is our duty to demand again, equality
before the electoral law, and, to that effect, we request that we be granted to be
heard.

With all consideration
P. ALBIZU CAMPOS
President of the Nationalist Party

Albizu Campos is acclaimed upon making his

NATIONALISM AND THE ELECTORAL SITUATION 73

Gentlemen D. Luis Sánchez Morales and
D. Manuel F. Rossy, presidents of the
Senate and the Chamber respectively.
Gentlemen:

In fulfillment of the mandate that has been imposed on me by resolution
the National Board of the Nationalist Party of Puerto Rico, I have the
honor of addressing this communication to you to demand equality before the
electoral law to which our patriotic organization is entitled.
Nationalism has systematically refrained from entering into the
bitter controversy over the electoral law because that fratricidal struggle
benefits exclusively the North American interventor who
contemplates it smiling and with approval. Nationalism will always compromise
with its Puerto Rican countrymen rather than favoring in any
way, the policy of Yankee foreign intervention that seeks to
annihilate Puerto Rico.
We do not believe in the tactics of the parties with which we painfully
have to face, because those tactics are condemned by the very
experience of those same groups, which have exhausted their patience
in a sterile diplomacy for the Government of Estados Unidos to
respect our sovereignty.
Our country is in ruins. It has lost in thirty-two years more than
seventy percent of its wealth that has passed to Yankee interests
by virtue of the monopoly they have of our market, which forces us
to buy theirs under the conditions they want to impose on us. And to
sell them ours in the terms that are pleasing to them.
We call attention to the fact that the high purchasing power that
Puerto Rico accuses and that allows it to occupy the position of the fifth
market for Estados Unidos in the world, and the first in América
Latina, is not based on real income, but on the displacement of
our farmers, industrialists and merchants, who are buying

not with profits derived from capital, but with the remnant of those
capitals after their forced liquidation by virtue of the public
auction of the assets to satisfy the contribution burden that falls
only on the natives of the country, as is proper in every
regime of foreign intervention.

That system respects not even the humblest home, and day after day
we have to contemplate the effect of that silent war of the public
auction, which throws the entire Puerto Rican family to the side of the roads. No well-intentioned government would allow such a
situation, because the contributory power can never be exercised to
the extermination of the ancestral home, as happens in Puerto
Rico; the exercise of that power is permissible for the promotion of the
economic independence of the home.

No people can stand idly by to watch the proletarian tide rise
that ascends with indifference, conspiracy or cowardice
own.

The social and cultural ills inflicted on the Puerto Rican nation
during the Yankee intervention are too clear to require
more analysis.

That is why, and based on the tragic experience of our
countrymen who do not militate in our ranks, that nationalism has arisen
to give a definitive halt to the Yankee government that sucks the blood of
the Homeland. Our program is clear as soon as we receive the suffrage
of the Puerto Rican majorities will immediately declare the
celebration of the Constituent Convention of the Republic.

We do not recognize the validity of the Yankee regime, because it is based on
the nullity of the Treaty of Paris. On the date of its signature by the mother
Homeland España, Puerto Rico had international personality, and no
treaty of España could oblige us without the express consent of the
Insular Parliament, and that indispensable requirement for its validity was not
obeyed. In other words, Puerto Rico was not a tradable thing and could not be the object of traffic, not only by virtue of the principles of
natural law, but by mandate of the strictest positive law
that informs the life of relationship between civilized nations.

We resort to peaceful means to effect the change of
regime because it is a norm that must be respected in the contemporary
age. Modern empires are obliged to respect the
independence of the intervened countries when the national will
shows itself unshakable. Empires consult their convenience, and this
is never found in provoking the revolution that wreaks havoc on their
interests. It has been ten years since the Nationalist Party was constituted. Has
resorted to the elections of the year 1924, in about ten municipalities and
the year 1928 only in Caguas, under the present current electoral law,
made under the direction of the head of the intervention in the year 1924, Mr.
Towner, and approved unanimously at the suggestions of the same.

governor. The purpose was to prevent any
nationalist sentiment in the country.
Currently the Party has registered general candidacies
by convention, but, according to the current interpretation, it does not have
representation in the Insular Electoral Board, and in the colleges
will have only an observer with voice but without vote, if the
current law is respected, which is being interpreted by the Superintendent of
Elections, Mr. Terry, as is natural in a North American
official, in the most restrictive sense in what concerns the
vital interests of the Nationalist Party.
We have been contemplating the electoral picture with patience while waiting
for this legislative assembly. The head of the occupation, as befits the one
who commands, has submitted to you the draft "law" that does not take into
consideration, ex professo, as was to be expected the position of party
by convention to which nationalism has arrived.
We are aware that if you do not accept the will already expressed
by the Governor, the tacit will of the Yankee Congress is expected
that defines in advance who should triumph in the elections. You do not have
legislative powers because you stumble with the absolute veto or with the
annulment of your acts by the Yankee Congress. It is yours, without
limitations, however, the legislative initiative, and it is to be expected that,
as Puerto Ricans, you accept only a law that guarantees to the
patriotic cause of nationalism that does not dispute anything from you unless
it is the sacrifice to constitute a free Homeland for all the
Puerto Ricans.
In international life, nationalism is what is representative; internally, it is the spur that obliges the invader to cede the fruit that does not fall into
our hands, but in the skirt of the parties that represent the middle ground. Nationalism is the only thing that belongs to every Puerto Rican.
Demands from you respect for your most sacred right: the
independence of the Homeland.
With all consideration,

P. ALBIZU CAMPOS
President,
Nationalist Party

A PROCLAMATION OF THE NATIONALIST PARTY
AROUND THE DATE OF TOMORROW FRIDAY 23
OF SEPTEMBER 74

The Puerto Rican nationality celebrates on September 23, 1932,
sixty-four years of baptism. On such a day of the
unforgettable 68, the liberators of Lares baptized with their blood the
marvelous body of the nation.
An something of perfumed vestment travels through our recollections
similar to a bugle call of religious faith, exalted by the ties
indissoluble of the concept of free Homeland. As if from the amphitheater
of immortality an effluvium of perfect joy softens our Calvary
and illuminates our decorum.
It is that the liberators, in the capacity of immortals, sweeten the life of
oppressed peoples establishing themselves as guardians of the code of
honor. Where the greatest number of illustrious dead rests lies the
most vigorous national dignity, because peoples are sustained by
the glory of their illustrious dead.
Puerto Rico, the only country in América that has produced holiness
and unfortunately the only country in América that suffers the most degrading of
enslavement, is sustained spiritually, thanks to the Olympic feat
of Lares, which marked, to the pride of generations to come, the
genesis of the most tender republic of América.
And pure men and wise men were the patriarchs of 68;
pure as the essence of supreme pain and wise as the beauty
of the Almighty. They were inspired by God and produced infinite
redeeming faith.
Thus they lived and thus they taught to live a life of purest liberties
and of wise fulgurations, abominating the men diminished
by betrayal and demanding purity and wisdom from those who
guarded the sacred dough of Homeland independence.
Sixty-four years of reactionary politics have passed, in which
sacrilege, erected in the name of beatitudes, betrayed the sublime
teaching of the liberators; sixty-four years of concealed
hypocrisy in the market of dishonor.
But the zeal of the illustrious dead arose in time. Vibrated in the
sky of human redemptions the birth of the nationalism of
Puerto Rico under whose flag, which is the flag of the Homeland, are
sheltered the children of the liberators of 68; thus ensuring the

precious custody of Puerto Rican liberties.
The nationalism of Puerto Rico, depositary in América of the faith of
the liberators, celebrates on September 23, 1932 the festival of
patriotism in Lares, cradle of the first heroes of independence.
To this festival of the spirit invites all Puerto Ricans who have not
mortgaged their souls to treason, wishing to contribute with the devotion
of the good believers in the religion of the Homeland to the homage that is
paid to the fathers of national history.
Given in San Juan, Puerto Rico, on the twenty-first day of the month of
September, sixty-fourth year of the proclamation of the republic.

P. ALBIZU CAMPOS
President of the Nationalist Party of
Puerto Rico

Rafael Rivera Matos
General secretary
