PROCLAMATION OF THE NATIONALIST PARTY ON
THE OCCASION OF SEPTEMBER 23rd.
PILGRIMAGE TO LARES. PROCLAMATION OF THE NATIONALIST
PARTY ON THE OCCASION OF SEPTEMBER 23rd,
DAY OF THE HEROES AND MARTYRS OF
INDEPENDENCE.

SECOND NATIONAL ASSEMBLY
Within a few short days, the sixty-third anniversary of the
maximum feat in the Homeland's annals will be commemorated, in which the national
sentiment of independence translated into deeds and the Puerto Rican people, from
the heroic mountains of Lares, inscribed September 23rd among the decorous dates of
the world. The emancipatory movement of Puerto
Rico, which the Nationalist Party embodies today, is not a
romantic movement; philosophical and juridical ideas serve as sublime
buttresses, but it has its authentic ascendancy in an epic day
baptized with blood and is cemented in the categorical devotion of the
good sons of this land for the cause of the unpostponable restoration of
the republic.
History attests that the independence of Puerto Rico, like
all the redemptive ideals of humanity, has its heroes and its
martyrs, and nothing will purify the collective conscience as much as
meditation on that immaculate sacrifice, which has been and will
always be above all profanations, in the very immortal setting
of its glorious apotheosis.
The Nationalist Party will therefore sponsor again this year the
patriotic national pilgrimage to the sacred land of Lares, which is
the august tabernacle of the Homeland. Our Party is the same as last
year by reason of the purity and integrity of its ideology,
invariable and immutable as the truth, but today it presents itself crowned
by success, on the summits, not of the colonial power that it despises, but
of the love and recognition and respect of its own and strangers, which
is the only thing that interests it.
Even the ambient ideas of the current world favor us. The
growth of international consciousness is raising the
relations between States, which were traditionally
resolved by force, to the plane of law more and more each day. The
absolute freedom of the powers is increasingly curtailed when it violates the rights of other States.
Peoples, like individuals, have already attained the right to
recognition of their juridical personality. The enslavement of countries
cedes before the concept of the family of nations. Even the
mere name of "colony" is reprehensible, and Puerto Rico demands its entry into the
society of nations.
We must always bear in mind, however, that victory for the
cause of independence depends exclusively on the national will
that reaches the sacrifice to impose justice and right.
Let the entire Puerto Rican nation attend the second national assembly
that will be held in Lares next September 23rd to render
a tribute of honor and gratitude and at the same time of solidarity and
atonement to the leaders of '68, who ignited in Puerto Rico, before
the Cubans in Yara, the holy fire of the freedom of the Homeland,
making the radiance of Bolívar's sword shine in our sky.
Given in the city of San Juan Bautista de Puerto Rico on the 15th day
of September of 1931, year 63 of the proclamation of the republic.
The National Board, Nationalist Party of Puerto Rico.

P. Albizu Campos, President

Manuel Rivera Matos, General Secretary

THE ACTS CELEBRATED BY THE NATIONALIST PARTY
IN THE CITY OF LARES ON THE TWENTY-THIRD OF SEPTEMBER
CURRENT

ALBIZU CAMPOS SPEAKS
When the President of the Nationalist Party was announced to take the floor, the music played La Borinqueña while the public, standing, paid delirious applause that lasted for several minutes.
"The human tendency is to be parasitic. Only a spiritual conception of life elevates us to the offering of what we possess for a just cause. Our people in their moral decadence is a parasitic people. But this apotheosis is not a parasitic act. We do not come here to raise a myth, nor to live a past glory. It is very easy to speak of the glories of others, but that does not make the speaker glorious. We come here as an act of consecration to the principles that informed the men of '68.
"We love and adore the Homeland as a goddess. The last
particle of our soil is a sacred thing and we will not allow foreign hands to profane it. But higher than that cult is the cult of our
progenitors and we will not allow foreign hands to profane it. They
represent the symbolism of the life we possess and of our
greatness before Eternity. Peoples who do not know their
progenitors, are buried peoples, are dead peoples. We, by
remembering their memory today, turn out to be unworthy successors of their
memory. Peoples are ungrateful because men are ungrateful; thus
our people do not know the descendants of those great
men. And we have to honor their memory in their very flesh".
The speaker then proceeded to make an analysis of the transcendence of the
personality. "The problem of Puerto Rico," he said, "is the problem of
its personality. There is no responsibility where there is no exercise of
powers. A people governed by a foreign power is an
irresponsible people."
He said that Puerto Rico was in the conditions of a madman who does not
have personality before the law for being an irresponsible entity and a tutor is appointed to administer his assets.
"The empire says to the colony: "I exercise power because you are an
incapacitated person". And it disposes of all lands and goods as a tutor.
Puerto Rico currently appears before the world as a people of
imbeciles who have no capacity to resolve their own affairs. To
enjoy rights, one must have personality and speak with the other
powers as equals".
The speaker analyzed the consequences of the denial of
personality in all orders, especially in the educational order.
He said that the empire established the educational system that suited its
purposes and therefore took care that the truth was not told in the
schools.
The speaker also addressed the religious problem. He said that no
religion in our country could be an enemy of our Independence;
that no one, in the name of Catholic or Protestant, could fight for the cause of
our freedom. He stated that Puerto Rico before the Holy See was a
independent nation that enjoyed ecclesiastical sovereignty. That in
its day the Nationalist Party would send a commission to the Vatican
to raise the case of Puerto Rico, He stated that all religious institutions had to be nationalized.
As this year the mass celebrated last year in honor of the heroes of '68 was not on the program, Mr. Albizu Campos personally addressed the Rev. Martínez, of that parish. "Last year," he said, "a Te Deum was sung in the church of this town and the
Rev. Martínez delivered the most eloquent speech in the assembly that
we gave in this same theater. Reverend Martínez revealed himself that
afternoon as a true Puerto Rican. Reverend Martínez owes
an explanation to his people as to why that mass has not been celebrated. He
must explain what secret coercions have prevented the perfume of a prayer to the martyrs of our Independence from rising today".
The speaker ended by saying that that pilgrimage was an act of
atonement to the heroes of '68. Mr. Albizu Campos was carried on
shoulders by the multitude, closing the assembly in the midst of delirious enthusiasm.

THE NATIONALIST PARTY PROCLAIMS NINE
DAYS OF MOURNING ON THE OCCASION OF THE DEATH OF
DON PEDRO ANGLERÓ, SURVIVOR OF THE GRITO
DE LARES
Every time a free man disappears, the Homeland trembles and the
future clouds over: the one, feeling in its most intimate essences the
painful onslaught of Destiny, and the other, suffering the orphanhood of the
constitutive virtues of hope. Hence, when manhood descends from its august pedestal obeying iron and eternal laws, it is
necessary to staunch the wound with a vision of eternity, for otherwise
the sense of spiritual harmony will succumb to the weight of misfortune.
The heroes of the great odyssey of Lares made the heart of their Homeland beat
because those venerable trunks had taken root in the depths of the Puerto Rican Spirit.
From then on, the Homeland, vilified in hours of joy
by the detractors of the Ideal, kept the ashes of its glorious dead very deep in its bowels, sure that the enemies of
Liberty, for lacking the vision of the eternal, are not in the
disposition to respect the sacred ashes of the dead.
But today, shaken the very essence of the Homeland by the death of the
survivor of the odyssey of Lares, don Pedro Angleró, at the age of
one hundred and ten years, it seems to us that the leading figures of the Revolution
of '68 make their appearance on the proscenium of their people and impose
silence, with the moral authority that induced them to defend, on a not distant
day, the reason of the ideals and the transcendence of libertarian thought.
Don Pedro Angleró has died today, in the Barrio Obrero, of this city of San Juan
Bautista, for physical life, at the moment in which
the Homeland, grieved and bleeding, weeps the absence of her true sons and
sinks into the despair of historical fatalism. A
centennial hero has died, in whose veins beat the redemption of a people
subjected by the power of force and by the denial of some of its
sons. The exalted banner of the Borincan epic has fallen, and, to
fall, with the sadness of the great and the happiness of the beautiful of
the soul, has risen with the elegance of the martyrs who always rise above the tabernacle, being in communion with the perfume of
sacrifice.
Above the tabernacle of disillusionments has jumped this noble
defender of morality and this priest of justice; over the
tabernacle that indifference hoists in hours of negative potentiality,
to impose the beheading of faith and decree that the vital force of
Right resides in the imposition of inequality and not in the
consciousness of men.
Don Pedro Angleró has died for physical life; but as in
every action of freedom two vital currents converge, the physical and the
moral, don Pedro Angleró, by losing the vibration of matter, has
seized the sources of morality, the plant of faith that he
sowed in the living furrow of the Homeland, will be cultivated with zeal and affection
by the noble hand of the present generation. And it will never die
because the dream crystallized in the heat of Eternity cannot die.
Before the pain that overwhelms the consciences of free
Puerto Rican men before the disappearance of this knight of the Ideal, the
Nationalism of Puerto Rico, is moved in its depth of purity and
patriotic love and pays the noble champion of feeling the tears that
perhaps all of Puerto Rico will have to shed before the venerable ashes of
their dead.
The nationalism of Puerto Rico, through this message of
mourning and in accordance with the illustrious lineage of don Pedro Angleró,
AESTHETIC MAN of the Borincan dream, proclaims nine days of
national mourning and orders the National Boards and sub-National Boards of
Puerto Rico that during these nine days dedicated to rendering religious homage to the memory of the noble man, the Flag
of the Homeland be flown at half-mast and that they impose on themselves the Pain that the fall of a Man who had the merit of being a true Man produces in us.
Given in San Juan de Puerto Rico, today, the sixteenth day of
October of 1931, and year 64 of the Proclamation of the Republic.

THE NATIONAL BOARD

P. ALBIZU CAMPOS
President

M. RIVERA MATOS
General Secretary

THE UNITED STATES HAS A DUTY TO FULFILL
WITH US
AND THAT IS TO WITHDRAW FROM OUR COUNTRY - IT IS THE
ONLY WAY TO DEMONSTRATE RESPECT TO
THE LAW OF NATIONS - ALBIZU CAMPOS
On Monday morning we visited the top leader Dr. Pedro Albizu
Campos in his office at the central offices of the Nationalist Party, who at that moment was extremely busy,
answering the numerous correspondence from the interior and abroad
coming from countless organizations, co-religionists and friends
inside and outside the nation.
Upon noticing our presence in his office he immediately
extended his firm right hand and with his proverbial smile inquired: "what
news is there?" "None, Don Pedro", we answered, "aside from the
ones that you can provide us. Do not forget that the readers of LA
NACION are anxiously awaiting your announced declarations".
-Ask, then - he replied.
-What do you tell us about the current political moment?
-"I already had the occasion to officially expose from the nationalist
tribune in Guayama our attitude in relation to the present
political panorama of the country.
-"As we publish in another part of this edition the
review of the act of Guayama we immediately formulated another
question.
-How will the United States Congress consider the project of
the elective governor of Mr. La Guardia, Democratic Representative for
New York in that legislative body?
-The nationalist movement is not interested in the consideration that
the United States Congress gives to any project that is submitted to
that legislative body. We maintain that the Puerto Rican nation is
absolute arbiter of its destiny, that the Treaty of Paris imposed
by the United States on Spain is null and void, in what refers to Puerto Rico, because
our country was not a contracting party in that treaty; neither did Spain have
powers to cede Puerto Rico nor the United States to accept it in
cession.
By virtue of the Autonomic Charter, which constituted a treaty between
the Mother Homeland and the island of Puerto Rico, our sovereignty
was recognized internationally and it was the duty of every nation
to respect it, starting with the United States.
When Nationalism rises energetically for the vindication
of its sovereignty, the invading power intends to disorient public opinion
to protect its usurpation and its dispossession by imposing
concessions to the oppressed country that perpetuate the foreign oppressor regime.
Liberalism has its insolences. Let us not forget, the pretended
liberals of all empires; they have always been imperialists who have
suggested the most subtle means to complete foreign absorption.
It is preferable for a nationality, victim of a military occupation,
a frank and decisive despotism that serves as an incentive and that awakens
indigenous resistance, to a veiled and liberal despotism that anesthetizes the national conscience.
The United States has a duty to fulfill with us. And it is
to withdraw immediately from our country. It is the only way to
demonstrate respect to the law of nations.
Immense damage has that invading nation caused to our
nationality, by depriving it of the exercise of its sovereignty and subjecting it to the
most barbaric and inhuman economic exploitation that history knows.
If any North American legislator wants to do some good to his
Homeland he should propose the respect for the right of others. If the representative
La Guardia wants us to deal with his projects, let him propose
to his country the immediate withdrawal of the North American armed forces
from our land, and the payment of reparations that they owe us for the immense damages caused in our country during 33 years of North American military occupation.
"The apparently liberal manifestations of Mr. La Guardia
are directed especially to the Puerto Rican colony of New York
to obtain from it the votes that Mr. La Guardia needs to
sustain himself in power.
"The National Board has given instructions to the New York Board
to enlighten our compatriots in that modern Babel to refrain from the internal struggles in that country so that they do not let their feelings be exploited by any North American politician; that their only
duty is to organize to restore the Republic of their Homeland.
-What significance do you attribute to the revolutionary movement
of the Philippines?
Fearing the Philippine revolution the North American government by Law
of the year 1917 committed itself to recognizing the Independence of the Philippine Islands (as soon as there was a stable government) and in the opinion of the government of the United States, there has not yet been "a stable government"
in the Philippines nor will there ever be despite the fact that the Philippine government is of
North American making.
"The Filipinos have been waiting fourteen years with this promise".
"In that period North American interests have been becoming stronger.
The conservative sentiment has been stimulated in
important nationalist sectors of that archipelago to such a point that the eminent statesman Roxas, more or less ten months ago, has raised
the flag of non-cooperation adopting tactics very similar to those of Puerto Rican nationalism.
"The Filipinos see clearly that the moment is most opportune for the
immediate suppression of the North American military occupation. United
States does not have today a friendly nation in the entire Universe,
because its policy of absorption has been unmasked. The
North American power is going through an internal crisis of revolutionary characters produced by the economic crisis, consequence of the loss of the markets.
"I firmly believe that the Filipinos will act to impose their
independence.
"The opportunity that the Puerto Rican nation has is identical to the
one that the Philippine nation has. Our duty is to act immediately.

THE RADIO CORPORATION OF PORTO RICO', SUBSIDIARY
OF THE INTERNATIONAL TELEGRAPH & TELEPHONE CO.,
REFUSES TO TRANSMIT POLITICAL SPEECHES

CENSORSHIP ON NATIONALISM PROPAGANDA.
DECLARATIONS OF THE PRESIDENT OF THE PARTY DON
PEDRO ALBIZU CAMPOS

"For some time influence has been exerted on the company
"Radio Corporation of Porto Rico" subsidiary of the "International
Telegraph and Telephone Co." for the radio transmission of nationalist speeches to be suspended.
It is absolutely certain that the North American government wanted to
suppress our propaganda through the airwaves and it is also
absolutely certain that the pro-Yankee elements yearned for the same
purpose.
We must call attention to the fact that in the United States freedom of speech through the airwaves does not exist. What has been
called indirect censorship prevails there, which is a formal admission that
freedom of speech or thought does not exist in that country. In the
Yankee Congress a bill is being considered to suppress
those restrictions of thought and of right.
Under a regime of force like the one that prevails in Puerto Rico, under
this North American military occupation, no right exists and only
a mere tolerance in the emission of thought, because the North American power trusts in its strength and in the dominion of the press.
The propaganda through the wireless way produced national and
international effects and has forced the invader to unmask himself, imposing
a censorship.
The excuse that the "Radio Corporation of Porto Rico", subsidiary
of the "International Telegraph and Telephone Co." gives is trivial. To allege
that a thought emission so vital for Puerto Rico is suppressed,
to dedicate its apparatus to commercial announcements and to news reports of the races of the Hippodrome, is to confess that they are acting under the
coercion of the North American government and in intelligence with the oppressive policy of that government, especially when those radio transmissions represented a very substantial income for the company.
They intend to besiege us by air since we are by sea and land.
