IN PUERTO RICO A PLAN IS PROPOSED FOR THE ANNEXATION
OF THAT COUNTRY' TOGETHER WITH CUBA AND HAITI TO THE UNITED
STATES<sup>6</sup>

A PUERTO RICAN ALSO PROTESTS

In addition to this vibrant and magnificent protest by the Cuban Consul,
who has known how to come to the defense of our dignity, a distinguished Puerto Rican,
Mr. P. Albizu Campos, responded to Mr. Tous's manifesto
with noble virility.
"Convinced Mr. Tous -says Mr. Albizu Campos- that
the Americans do not esteem us as equals despite citizenship,
but always desirous of seeing us tied to the Yankee imperial chariot,
he advises our acceptance in fact and contrary to right and to the effect
he sustains a project of amendment to the Constitution, to admit
Puerto Rico as a "state" with representation but without vote in
Congress, except in its own matters.
Such an amendment would well reflect the American attitude, but it is not
moved by an ingenuity that allows to unmask its hypocrisy.
Even if the amendment were feasible, it is so humiliating that
we accept a position of inequality, that that is enough to reject it
outright.
The most painful thing of all this is that Mr. Tous is not satisfied
with the sinking of his Homeland, but offers the United States a
plan that would destroy the aspirations for the independence of the great Filipino people,
and that constitutes a gratuitous attack on our brother peoples
in the race, Cuba, Haiti and Santo Domingo, who with sacrifice,
the only saving thing, freed themselves from the clutches of the conquest.
Why instead of facilitating the annexation of Cuba, Haiti and Santo
Domingo, does he not protest the vandalistic outrage of which the
Dominican people are victims, the promiscuous slaughter of thousands of Haitians,
of the failed attacks against the sovereignty of Cuba after having
sworn to defend it? He does not protest. We form a chain with those
untamed peoples, whose example we must imitate, and since we
sink with pleasure, he believes that we must drag them also to the
same abyss. This attitude is logical, after preaching so much to this
unhappy people that it is not capable of anything, comes the
conviction of moral incapacity to resist the brutal attack of the
strong, and he forgets that these men in other lands ARE NOT
MADE OF GELATIN. It is dishonorable that Puerto Ricans
serve as an instrument to facilitate the plan premeditated by
American statesmen, to annex their Latin neighbors, with the
economic penetration, disorganizing their institutions and finally
applying the garrote of military force".

THE DISSOLUTION OF THE UNIONIST PARTY
AND PUERTO RICAN NATIONALISM"

The Puerto Rican Alliance, a fusion of the Parties that were
called Unionist and Republican, has placed the country in the dilemma
of submitting to the dictatorship of the plutocracy or to the dictatorship of
demagoguery. The electoral right has been destroyed; the directory will decide
who we must vote for in the next elections if we are to allow
it to exercise the will it pretends.

When the electoral right is destroyed, the gesture is made of
favoring women's suffrage. Dictatorships have used
many subterfuges to impose themselves, but we never believed that they would have
to use a woman's skirt as a shield,

Why a dictatorship in Puerto Rico? This is imposed only in
moments of great crisis and to obtain or protect
fundamental collective rights when all the ordinary means
of government have failed. It is an extremely exceptional measure and is tolerated and
sanctioned by an imperative necessity.

Our people are accused of not being prepared for self-government.
Their rulers deny them the opportunity to prepare themselves and exercise
over them a veiled imperialism. To culminate their humiliation, their own sons
want to assume over them the gesture of dictators.

Each political party in Puerto Rico should be a school of
democracy. Apparently, it is an inconvenience to certain leaders of the
public opinion.

What purpose does the dictatorship pursue? In the definitive agreements of
the assemblies there is talk of the agrarian problem, the problem of schools
and other problems, for whose solution a
dictatorship is not justified because today we have powers to solve those problems.
We are also told that the alliance will direct all its efforts to the
consideration of full self-government to achieve the sovereignty of the
people of Puerto Rico within the sovereignty of the United States.
The authors of this phrase have explained to us that achieving full
self-government is confined to obtaining the approval of the bill
pending consideration before the United States Congress, under
which Puerto Rico may elect its own governor in
1928. But that same project provides that that governor may be
removed by the President of the United States. The governor thus
elected, will feel responsible to the President of the United States and not to the
people who granted him their representation. We will achieve with his
election putting one more puppet at the disposal of Yankee imperialism
in Puerto Rico, which will thus be able to escape the responsibility for its bad
government and the sons of our country will bear it. Wise is the

29

Yankee policy that knows how to find good instruments for its execution!
The debated phrase "sovereignty within sovereignty" some tell us
that it means the federal "state" and others that it means
free associated state. Its authors offer interpretations that
constitute an antithesis. It carries death within it. Of course, it cannot
refer to the federal "state", because it is neither a state nor sovereign. It is
a mere province of the American nation. As the other interpretation
is so contrary to this, we come to the conclusion that the phrase does not
mean anything.

It is a veil to cover up the colonialism that wants to be implanted with the
consent of the men in whom we placed our
trust.
* The Department of War, which disposes of life and property in Puerto Rico,
has just published an official report in which it makes
a complete narration of the policy of the United States in Puerto Rico
from the Spanish-American War until now. We are told clearly
that Puerto Rico will never be admitted into the American Union in
accordance with all the precedents followed until now. It is denied
also its right to constitute itself as a free nation. And cynically it is
affirmed that American citizenship for us is only an imperial
means to keep us subject to the Yankee mandate.
Our leaders seem resigned to the situation imposed on them by
American kaiserism, and for "convenience", they want to subject their
people to that will that is hostile to their happiness and dignity. Faithful to
the imposed mandate, they have constituted the Alliance to formalize the colonialism
already established. We have been told that the object is to sanitize the
political environment, to protect us against the socialist calamity, etc., etc., but
no one has believed this, with the exception of the people who are interested in the
constitution of the directory.

None of the militant parties will take care to defend our
collective personality and our inalienable right to constitute a
free people among the free peoples of the world. The ideal of independence has died among the
militant parties, our people have been toyed with. Its great majority is
independentista to the bone.

We offer the most exceptional spectacle: the people are the action,
their leaders, the reaction; the people, the vanguard, their leaders, the
impedimenta. All the effort of our leaders seems to have
been limited to organizing hosts that, after being organized, have taken
fright and have decided to surrender with their army in order not to incur the
malevolence of the enemy.

For the Unionist Party, whose soul was always nationalist, what has happened is
death. It has been formally decreed by stipulating that the Alliance
will be for an indefinite time and that all its
official bodies will be suspended. No one may convene the Union of Puerto
Rico to any meeting after this agreement.

"The preservation of the insignia, the name, and the personality", is
a skillful formula to maintain the cohesion of the hosts so as not to
see the directory reduced to a pretentious skeleton. The truth is
that the Union of Puerto Rico and the so-called
30

Republican Party has been dissolved and a new party dominated by the
plutocracy has been constituted.
No people could expect any benefit from this combination and
much less ours that, excessively trusting, places its trust
everywhere. The only goodness of said movement is the
resurgence of Puerto Rican Nationalism. The new nationalism
will be true. It will watch over the economic well-being of the working classes,
it will bring the lands back into Puerto Rican hands, it will not
allow the imposition of an exotic culture, and it will not cease its effort
until it vindicates the Homeland dignity.
This painful imposition has been invested with the language that is used
to consecrate the great ideals. Strip the proposition of that
beautiful attire and you will find the macabre figure of an audacity
committed by sons and the tranquility, of reconstruction. (sic). It will not be the
plutocratic Alliance, nor will it be the demagoguery that will impose itself on
this people. All of Puerto Rico will rise up with the patriotic ideal of a
pure nationalism and will punish at the polls the men who have
violated its trust. The Homeland is indestructible and will survive all the
impositions of its own or of strangers.

PEDRO ALBIZU CAMPOS
Ponce, Puerto Rico, May 12, 1924

OUR INTERVIEWS

We went to see Lcdo. Albizu Campos, at his law office in the city of
Ponce, where we talked extensively with him, about the
political topics that in this hour of concerns and thoughts worry the
world; Albizu is deeply documented, and he is also in
direct relations with the men and associations abroad who
at this moment are interested in the right of the peoples, and in the
justice of humanity.
Albizu Campos is a nervous and dynamic spirit that acts
silently, without stridencies, but placing his absolute faith in the solid creed of his ideal. His precise and forceful conversation cracks
like a whip, it phosphoresces, sometimes it burns like a flame. He is one of
our most distinguished tribunes. His oratory is not sprinkled with
rhetoricisms but abounds in ideas and virile exaltations. When
he speaks to the people, his word takes on strength and flashes of fire,
shaking the masses in a vortex of gusts of tempest. Then, the
blizzard becomes soft and when he speaks to the heart, even the
most recalcitrant multitudes, meditate. Albizu Campos is a reality
unquestionable, but his great promising harvest is already in season. The
ears of corn are turning yellow, while they are weighed down with pure maternal feelings.
Having finished our interview, we asked for his portrait.
-I don't have a portrait -he told us.
-But, we need it.
-It doesn't matter, publish the interview without it. It is not necessary.
-Let's go to Valiente's studio.
The man became surly.
-It would be an outrage, -he protested.
Our gaze was running through the paintings of the office, while
we were talking. With a glance we found the object of our desire.
We already had the photograph. In a careless moment it would be ours. It would
also offer us the occasion to make known to our readers the
cordial reception that was given in the city of the South to José Vasconcelos
passing through to Santo Domingo, by this group of men who love Puerto
Rico above all things.
Here is some of the much that we talked with Pedro Albizu Campos.
-Where did you graduate as a lawyer?
-At Harvard University of New England.
-When you came to Puerto Rico, which political party attracted your
attention?
-None.
-We would like to tell the readers of "Poliedro" your personal
political process until its culmination as Vice-president of the

34

Nationalist party.
I was always a defender of the absolute independence of the Homeland.
I called the annexationist "republicans" renegades and the
"socialists" misguided and the parties they integrated, instruments of the imperialism of the United States and responsible in
great part for the destruction of our nationality. The
Unionist party despite its fifth base requesting the recognition of
our independence, did not interest me, because it was a party
frankly governmental and that cooperated directly to the work of
demolition directed from Washington instead of sustaining a
collective action that would allow us to conserve our wealth, as a
necessary base to establish our national sovereignty.
When Mont Reily arrived in Puerto Rico, of grateful memory for
many traitors, and who threw the unionists out of power, I suffered a grave
mistake in believing that, outside of the pain, a rebellious party would be organized. I confused the hubbub formed by those who had lost the
public posts with the true rebellion. Then I entered the
Unionist party. I was the only one who entered the struggle when the party
could not count on a single post. Everyone knows how it ignominiously surrendered to the imperial power. Despite that I continued
sustaining the ideal of independence in all the meetings and assemblies
of the party. When this one resolved to constitute with the Republican
party the Puerto Rican Alliance, I understood that the party of the majority
was definitely submitting to colonialism and I withdrew immediately from its
ranks.
My work in the Nationalist Party is known throughout the country.
-What about your trip to Hispanic America as Special Envoy
of Puerto Rican Nationalism?
-The Nationalist party, in its last assembly, imposed that
duty on me, and I hope to leave for the lands of Ibero-American promise at
the beginning of this year.
-What plan would you draw up for Puerto Rico to achieve the right to
enjoy its sovereignty in the concert of free peoples?
-Intense economic organization, conservation of our culture
and Hispanic civilization, suppression of yacophile influences, non-
cooperation with the North American imperial government, passive resistance
and preparation of our people for the international struggles that are approaching.
-Do you believe that the United States represents a danger for
Hispanic America or on the contrary is it the point through which the new civilization must enter for us?
-The United States is the danger for all of humanity, and
especially for Ibero-America because of its absorbent policy.
In Europe and in America there is a civilization of origin
Greco-Roman and of which we Puerto Ricans are direct heirs
because of our link with Spain. That civilization has degenerated in
the United States. The knowledge that it has offered to man, the
North Americans have dedicated it to exploit the immigrants and the
blacks within their borders, and now they intend to continue the same work

with the neighboring nations. Lacking responsibility in human relations, for them all means are good if it is a matter of increasing
their material wealth. And that is not civilization but barbarism armed with
some knowledge of civilization. The North Americans have nothing
to offer the world for its improvement unless it is the
destruction of their empire.
-Don't you believe in the possibility of a new world war? If
it were so, what do you think would be the destiny of the Antilles?
-It is inevitable. The common enemy is the United States. The battlefield
will be our waters in front of Panama, Europe, Ibero-America and
Asia, will not tolerate being reduced to the vassalage that the United States intends to impose on them, be it by direct force against defenseless peoples
of their territories or by economic penetration.
The Antillean Archipelago occupies a privileged position in the
world geography. It becomes essential to throw the United States out of its territories
and to consolidate them under a confederation with
sufficient naval power to resist any revenge, because I have no
doubt of the decision that the next war will have. This will not
end until the North American empire is reduced to impotence.
-What concept do you have of the Puerto Rican literary movement
in relation to our politics?
-Almost all of our literati have extinguished their numen in the shadow
of the budget, or they believe themselves completely defeated. The letters tell us only of pain, uncertainty, deception, suicide or
conventionalism.
There are the noble exceptions of those men who know for what
God has given them the privileged faculty of seeing clearly and expressing
gallantly. They, although few, invoke the irresistible force that
emanates from our Hispanic civilization and culture and from our
racial traditions. They wield the verb with courage and inspiration so that our people may regain faith in their own greatness and prepare for the
struggle for the vindication of their rights.
-Do you believe that our poets are doing something with their literature
for the cause of the ideal of independence?
-Almost nothing. When they do not contribute directly to its defeat with
bloodless verses that smell of suicide and dissolution. The poet who evokes in our people all their greatness has not yet emerged.
-Then, what orientations should the poets have to give
a strong contribution to those ideals?
-They must recover the value that inspires all creation so as not
to disfigure it by thinking of external conventionalism. They must not drown
their numen in their sterile subjectivism and love affair at odds with aesthetic expression. They must be the vanguard in the defense of their own dignity and
that of their Homeland, as well as of their Hispanic culture, so that they may
figure in the world Parnassus
-We would like to know what is your vision of the future of Puerto Rico
in the face of the United States.
-Our Homeland will be a free and sovereign nation against the will
of the United States. Its independence without limitations as well as that of the

36

nations of the Caribbean and of Central America under the pernicious influence
of the United States currently, will be imposed as a
universal necessity that sustains the balance between the nations, and a state of
law between them in this hemisphere.
-What do you think is the destiny of Hispanic America in the
concert of the great nations?
-Our Iberian nations are the nations of the future. Europe and
North America intend to secure privileged positions within them
with a view to future events. A harmonious understanding between
the Ibero-American nations would immediately reestablish the
international equilibrium destroyed by the Great War that gave the
North Americans the economic hegemony of the world, thus creating the
most serious danger with which humanity has stumbled until now.
These are in broad strokes some of the ideas poured out by Albizu
Campos in the course of our long conversation. Naked and frank,
they enclose between the lines serious and profound commentaries. We
meditate...

(From our Editor in Ponce, Mr. Maldonado)

In the "Hotel Meliá" we greet the Vice-president of the Nationalist Party, Lcdo. P. Albizu Campos. We ask him several
questions of political actuality, to which he answered immediately:
-What do you think, we ask him, about the manifestations that
Representative MacFadden 11 made to President Coolidge, with respect
to Puerto Rico?
"MacFadden has told the absolute truth. He is the only congressman
who has penetrated the conscience of this people", Lcdo.
Albizu Campos answered us.
"We Puerto Ricans are anti-American because we have to
combat a government that the only thing it serves in our land is
to tear out its riches, destroy its culture and reduce it to an amorphous,
despicable mass of day laborers".
"A strong people like the United States, with all the means to
make Puerto Rico happy, denies it the most elementary rights of life
and liberty, presenting itself in the crude form of the old empires
oppressors, who have deserved the curse of history".
-"What opinion have you formed about the cablegram addressed by
Mr. Barceló to the Resident Commissioner, Mr. Córdova Dávila,
asking him to confirm if MacFadden had really made the
declarations that are imputed to him?
"Sad message of disloyalty. We must be loyal even with our
enemies. It is of interest to Puerto Rico that the gentlemen of the colonial Legislature, manifest themselves with more civility, so that they can inform the
government of the United States the way of thinking and feeling of our
people".

The definitive definitions are the only base of permanent understandings
between friends and between enemies. That message contributes to
the disorientation of the North American people, who need to be
told frankly that their policy in this land and in other parts of the
world, has created deep hostility".

What scope do you give to the manifestations of Representative
Mac Fadden with respect to that "the North American government must adopt
measures to avoid that the pro-American feeling does not weaken in
Puerto Rico"?

"Puerto Rico has never been Yankee nor will it ever be. Every measure
that the government of the United States adopts to denature our
country, with the purpose of "Yankeeizing" it, assimilating it to annex it
definitively, will be rejected by the Puerto Ricans, consciously or
unconsciously, because there is an eternal conflict of race, of culture,
of civilization and of material interests between the people of Puerto Rico
and the people of the United States, harmonizable only on the basis of the only
understanding between defined nationalities, which is the mutual recognition of their independence, which postulates reciprocal treatment on the basis of equality restored by the law of nations and by the principles that inform our Western civilization.
"We Puerto Ricans will combat every executive, legislative
or judicial act of the United States in this land, because its only sanction is the
force of the United States, and is contrary to our will. North
America has a duty to fulfill with us, and with the
Ibero-American peoples, of which we are part, and it is to recognize the
full independence of Borinquen".
"And that duty will be imposed on it by the force of incontestable
international facts that are approaching to reestablish the equilibrium disturbed
by North American imperialism".
