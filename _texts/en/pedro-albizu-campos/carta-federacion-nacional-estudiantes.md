Aguas Buenas, Puerto Rico,
March 15, 1936

To Mr. Joaquín Rodríguez Benítez,
President of the National Federation of
Puerto Rican Students:

To Mr. José R. Lebrón Velázquez,
President of the National Federation of Students
of the High School;

To all the Noble and Courageous Student Body
of Puerto Rico:

The courageous and patriotic youth constitutes the sacred
posterity of the Fatherland.

It is the reserve that must be cared for as the source of our
eternal nationality.

It is the duty of adults to confront with courage and sacrifice
all patriotic problems, especially when it is expected to
offer life to make respect the existence and the Independence
of the Nation. In these precise moments of our
history, your parents are fulfilling their duty. Brave and
self-sacrificing, they serve as shields for their children against the excesses
of a despicable empire.

The youth should not abandon its classrooms. In its
privileged forum, it must wait serenely and alert for the moment of
fulfilling its patriotic duties.

Your organization instills terror in foreign despotism.

Keep it prepared and disciplined for the
decisive hour.

El Mundo, March 16, 1936, p. 1 and 4.

With the respect and emotion inspired by your solid
patriotic solidarity, I allow myself to advise you not to go on
any strike.

In the classrooms, your power is omnipotent.

The most powerful and brutal of empires cannot violate,
without serious consequences for its security, the sacred forum of the
student body.

Trust in the adults: in your parents and in your
older brothers. They will fulfill their duty in any
situation that Yankee imperialism may want to present us with.

Do not rush into the fight. In the national moral
restoration, adults cannot decline to the student body the
fulfillment of any patriotic duty.

Remain serene and alert.

Let your motto be: Courage and Sacrifice.

P. ALBIZU CAMPOS.
