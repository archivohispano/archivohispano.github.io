-Is that a quote from the statement sent by you?
-I ordered that a copy be sent to each newspaper.
-Did you sign the copies sent to the newspapers?
-I believe I must have signed them.
-And that, if you recall, is it a substantial copy?
-It is a substantial copy. I cannot assure it, but I believe that it is substantially the same thing.

-Yes.
-Could you tell us what it is?
-It is an enlistment card.

-Do you know by whose order those cards were printed?
-By my personal instructions.
-How many cards were printed?
-I believe around forty thousand.
-Could you tell us who printed them?
-I will not incriminate anyone here; but I want to say that it was people who do not belong to our organization.
-Yes.
-In Fajardo?
-Yes.
-In Loíza?
-Yes.
-In Arecibo?
-Yes.
-In Quebradillas?
-Yes.
-Could you tell me if Clemente Soto Vélez and Erasmo Velázquez had or did not have a connection with the enlistment office of Caguas?
-Well, I will not incriminate those boys. You have them prosecuted. It is unfair because they are accused.
-And for the same reasons you would not like to declare in regard to the other members?
-Yes, sir.
-How many members does the Partido Nacionalista currently have?
-Well, it would be impossible to calculate it.

-I want to say that they are not allowed to vote in anything related to the Partido Nacionalista and that they cannot perform any position in the Partido Nacionalista.
-If you would be so kind, I would like you to explain more definitively the word "desalentamos" [we discourage]. Are they prohibited from belonging to the Party?
-I want to say that we do not try to attract them to us. If they apply and come in good faith, we accept them.
-And what about the members of the Guardia Nacional?
-We discourage them, as well as those who serve in the Ejército de Estados Unidos.
-Of course, you do not encourage them?
-We cannot prevent that if a member of the Guardia Nacional, or of the Ejército de Estados Unidos of the regular army...
-As I understand you, you do not encourage them?
-We discourage them.
-Will you be able to calculate how many members of the Partido there are?
-If you allow me, I will give you the reasons to make things clear. Our criterion is that the political corruption prevalent in Puerto Rico is due to public employment, and that is why we are divided. We consider that the people should not fight for something as dirty or outrageous as the budget. The budget is the source of disturbance between the Gobierno de Estados Unidos and this community, the main source of disturbance. -Señor Albizu, I forgot to ask you. When you had these cards printed, who distributed them to the various municipal boards?
-I ordered the distribution.
-And were the recruits in each municipal board asked to give the information requested on those cards?
-Yes.
-And were they registered in the registration books?
-No, first they had to be members of the Junta Municipal before they could sign this card.
-As I see it, there is no place here for the signature.
-Can I explain this card to you? This card cannot be signed by anyone who has not been a nationalist for more than one year on the date of issuance of the card. This card was not issued for people who made their adhesion to the Partido Nacionalista during the last year.
-But in addition to filling out this card, did they not also sign one as a kind of enlistment book?
-I think that each Junta tried to keep its own record.
-Each Junta Municipal?
-Yes, but the thing consisted of signing the card.

-Could you tell me with how many members approximately does the Partido Nacionalista count in San Juan?
-In San Juan, around two thousand.
-And in Ponce?
-I believe that more than that.
-More than two thousand?
-Yes. It is a calculation made offhand.
-In Aguas Buenas?
-I believe that the entire community is Nationalist.
-How many are there in the community? I cannot say. But I suppose that there are approximately one thousand.
-I suppose then that in all those sites all those members of the party are members of the Ejército Libertador.
-No, because many of them came to the Party last year.
-I see. How many members joined the Partido Nacionalista during the past year?
-I suppose, this is a quick calculation. I do not like to give exaggerated figures; let us suppose, twenty thousand.
-That means that twenty thousand were members of the party a year ago.
-Yes.
-Those twenty thousand have been enlisted in the Ejército Libertador.
