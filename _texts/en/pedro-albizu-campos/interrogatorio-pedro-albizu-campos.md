Albizu Campos before the Grand Jury

Pedro Albizu Campos, under oath, declares as follows:

-What is your name?
-Pedro Albizu Campos.

-Were you summoned by a "subpoena duces tecum" to produce before this Grand Jury the documents listed in that subpoena, addressed to you as President of the Partido Nacionalista de Puerto Rico and of the Junta Nacional?
-Yes, sir.

-Have you brought those documents?
-No, sir.

-Do you refuse to produce them?
-I have an answer for that. I wish to clarify at length and say everything to the Grand Jury.

-Señor Albizu Campos has just stated that he wishes to clarify everything before the Grand Jury. It is appropriate for me to advise the Grand Jury and the witness that a witness has the privilege of not testifying against himself, that he cannot be compelled to testify against himself. I now wish to inform the witness that anything he declares may be used against him, and that the statements you make before this Grand Jury will be used against you.

-I do not know; I have not seen him.

-Did Corretjer consult you with regard to the production of those books?
-Yes, sir.

-Did you advise him not to produce them?
-I told him that if he did so he would incriminate himself in the case being pursued against him.

-Is there no case against him before this Grand Jury?
-He is under arrest. He was arrested and posted bail before the United States Commissioner. First of all I wish to protest against the words of the District Attorney. I am not evading responsibilities of any kind, and I am here to answer all questions honestly and frankly, and I am going to invite the District Attorney to follow in this proceeding the same one we followed before the United States Commissioner, because I suppose we are trying to arrive at the truth.

-Are you going to do what you can to help us even when your statements incriminate you?
-Yes, sir. I am a loyal, chivalrous, courteous, and honest man.

-If you wish to testify even though it incriminates yourself, you may do so, because you have waived your privilege of not testifying against yourself.
-Exactly. No technicalities.

-Why then do you not produce the records requested in the "subpoena duces tecum" addressed to you as President of the Partido Nacionalista de Puerto Rico? Do you base your refusal to produce the records on the privilege you have not to testify against yourself?

-His duties are to maintain correspondence with different institutions throughout the world. For some time this position was vacant since the death of the last Secretary of Foreign Relations, Monserrate Bonilla, who died about six months ago in Isabela, and the duties of this position are now being carried out by Señor Corretjer, who is the Secretary General, acting in his dual capacity as Secretary General and Secretary of Foreign Relations.

-You say that his duty is to communicate with all the [...]

-Puerto Rico exists as a republic, as a legal matter. Puerto Rico was recognized as an independent nation in 1897 by virtue of the Autonomic Charter of Puerto Rico. There was a distinction because we had a monarchical form of government. The term "Republic" refers to the form of government. Puerto Rico, by virtue of the Autonomic Charter granted by Spain in 1897, was recognized as a sovereign, free and independent nation, and relations between Puerto Rico and Spain, after the granting of the Autonomic Charter, were to be regulated by treaties on equal terms, and the Autonomic Charter is the first treaty that Puerto Rico has formed. It specifies relations, commercial relations, a treaty of friendship, commerce and navigation. It established what we call in international law a modus vivendi, that is, a preliminary agreement subject to modifications according to the convenience of the contracting parties. The terms of the Autonomic Charter are contained at length in this brief filed before the Supreme Court of the United States, for the consideration of the highest court of the United States of America.

-I have read in that motion that you give to understand that it is one of your theories that the Republic of Puerto Rico was established in 1868.
-Yes, sir, that is correct. The Republic of Puerto Rico was established in 1868.

-Can you explain to the Jury your theory in that regard?
-Yes, sir. In 1868 there was a revolution in Puerto Rico, which was part of a joint plan to liberate Cuba and Puerto Rico from the Spanish monarchy. It was a joint plan of Cuba, Puerto Rico, Santo Domingo and Haiti, and the central organization of that plan resided in the city of Puerto Plata in the Republic of Santo Domingo. The plan demanded joint military action against the monar[...] public of Puerto Rico has existed since 1868.

-As a legal matter, the Republic has existed since that date.

-I have here a bond that purports to be a bond of the Partido Nacionalista de Puerto Rico.
-Yes, sir.

-Your name is printed on this bond and signed as "President." Is this your signature?
-Yes, sir, it is my signature, a printed signature. I signed the original. The original signature was in my own handwriting. I signed as President of the Partido Nacionalista de Puerto Rico.

-Can you tell us who printed these bonds?
-I do not remember.

-How many of these bonds were put into circulation?
-Very few because I opposed their circulation. In 1932 some were issued with the purpose of raising funds for the nationalist movement, but I opposed their circulation as premature, because I believed it could be misinterpreted. In 1932, for the elections, certain local organizations of the Party requested that they be given an opportunity to raise some funds for the electoral campaign.

-You mean of the Partido Nacionalista?
-Of the Partido Nacionalista, and they were permitted to place some of them, very few.

-I note that the bond contains a statement giving the year 1930 as the year in which the bond is issued, and also[...] Not at this moment, but yes at any moment, because I do not know what the Junta Nacional may decide. These matters are considered by the Junta Nacional. In my opinion, the situation is favorable for the placement of these bonds. It was not so in 1932, because public opinion was very divided.

-Do you have bonds of this type available?
-Yes, sir.

-Printed?
-Yes, sir, and we will issue more if the situation were favorable.

-I note in this bond that it says: "First Loan of the Republic against the Treasury of the Republic of Puerto Rico" and that it will be paid five years after the international recognition of the Republic of Puerto Rico, and that its Treasurer (I suppose meaning the Treasurer of the Republic of Puerto Rico) will pay to the bearer of this obligation the sum of one dollar in gold, which was paid to the Treasurer of the Partido Nacionalista, for the purpose of reconstituting the Republic of Puerto Rico — if the Republic of Puerto Rico did not exist when these bonds were issued?
-Yes, sir, we could obligate the Treasurer of the Republic of Puerto Rico just as the United States in 1774 issued bonds before constituting the Republic. That is because, if the Partido Nacionalista were to succeed in restoring the Republic, the Republic would not fail to honor the obligations issued by the men who gave their lives and efforts to give a free country.

-The money was for the Partido Nacionalista?
-Yes, sir.

-The purpose is to prepare foreign nations and the people of the United States to understand the justice of the cause of independence of Puerto Rico and the convenience of Puerto Rico being recognized by the United States as an independent nation.

-[...] the people asked the Tsar to submit the matter to a judicial tribunal and the Tsar agreed. His advisors recommended that he submit this matter to a judicial investigation and an international council of jurists met in London in 1912, and this international council of jurists rendered a decision to the effect that once the personality of a nation is recognized, that personality cannot be revoked. Remember that a monarchical autonomy means a free, sovereign and independent nation. The autonomous system of government cannot prevail under the current American regime of military intervention in Puerto Rico because that term is inadequate, improper, because such a measure under the constitutional regime of the United States is frankly impossible. Either the community becomes a State of the Union, or it becomes a free country, or it is governed irresponsibly, as is the case of Puerto Rico. Well, the point that the Partido Nacionalista raises is this: that the Autonomic Charter that Spain granted to Puerto Rico recognizing its sovereignty could not be revoked by any subsequent action of Spain. It was binding on all free powers, because the recognition of the international personality of a nation is obligatory, because that is the basis of international relations, as the basis of the status of emancipation. If a minor is brought before a court and emancipated, and the next day the family and the court wish to revoke the decree by which the minor was emancipated, it cannot be done, because that is the contractual basis of society. The same occurs in international life and in international relations. And that is what happened in Puerto Rico. And by the Treaty of Paris, Spain had no authority to cede anything because it had nothing here and, consequently, the United States has no authority whatsoever in Puerto Rico, and that is why what we have here is a military intervention. The autonomous government functioned as an international entity. It had its own currency, its own postage stamps. -Well, the resolution did not have that scope. We had had what we call a cadets of the Republic organization since 1930, obligatory for all nationalists, and we were trying to make it obligatory for other nationalists, and at the convention in Caguas we tried to make it obligatory.

-Did you change the name "Cadets of the Republic" to "Ejército Libertador"?
