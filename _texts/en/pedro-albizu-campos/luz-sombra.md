TENTH ASSIGNMENT
The Supreme Court erred in upholding the ruling of the District Court of San Juan, affirming that the penalty was not unusual or unduly severe.
The defendant was deprived of a jury trial in the Court in said case.
However, the indictment was brought in the name of the People of Puerto Rico because, deprived of a jury trial under local statutes, a finding of guilt was a sure thing. Then, relegated to that far and distant place, the defendant would have had to suffer as the Prosecutor expected.
It is a sure thing that the jury would have found the defendant guilty.
A sentence of one year plus costs, imposed by judges who are supposed to be more free from prejudices and fears than juries, seems repulsive.
The judges owe their positions to political appointments and are at the mercy of the will of the Governor of Puerto Rico, representative in Puerto Rico of the power of the United States. Without a doubt, they did not feel free to act in this case.
The decision of the lower Courts should be reversed.
Respectfully submitted,

PEDRO ALBIZU CAMPOS
Attorney for the Accused-Appellant
In San Juan, Puerto Rico, on January 11, 1911.

Light and Shadow

Shadow, dreams, creation, here is the scale of Eternity.
God made everything from Nothing, that is, from Shadow. From it he also drew the light for human understanding of the creative secret; but the best mystical understanding is not possible unless the light extinguishes the retina and restores the empire of Shadow.

Shadow is the soul as a divine and immortal spark. In it is resolved the mystery of light and shadow as in God Almighty.
Twilight, lunar eclipse, solar eclipse, extinction of stars, disappearance of universes, restitution of the mystical shadow, creative matrix, which God will make luminous in his divine power, to individualize everything into beings that will always carry with them the perpetual memory of their gestational mother: Shadow.

Bodies dissolve and shadows perpetuate. Immortality is the eternal law.
God enjoys mutations in his own work, and he governs the elements with the internal form, that is, they will grow like his shadow. Morphology is a revelation.

Light as emerged from the arcane, by decree of the Omnipotent, is like the son: it will always project to its own mother, Shadow.
Initiation is in the understanding of Shadow. The shadows will speak to us and reveal the great secret; they will sing to us, and make audible the harmony of their divine concert.

For understanding of the All, we will extinguish the suns and the stars. We will re-enter the Genitoring Shadow.

By Pedro Albizu Campos
Río Piedras, February 11, 1935.

La Palabra, December 2, 1915, p.2. It was later reproduced in the magazine Criterio, April 1916, p. 14.

It is the day of consecration to the flag.
To fully enter into the climate of this illustrious date, Nationalism will wear its best gala attire, aware, as it is, of the transcendence of the apostolate.
From all horizons of the Nation, nationalist representations will come to this capital, contributing to the splendor of the civic festival.
The cadets of the Republic will render martial honors to the procerous figures whose deeds are commemorated and anointed hands will cover the tombs of the heroes with natural flowers.
As in previous times, the commemoration of April 16 will constitute a milestone of glory on the scale of Puerto Rican nationality. Being this the Day of Youth, the entire Nation will feel the joy of high things. Youth will speak in the name of national decorum and will swear again not to rest until making Puerto Rico a Free and Sovereign Republic.
The oath of Youth, pronounced in the presence of the Heroes, don José De Diego and Suárez Díaz, will stand out for its eloquent sanctity. It will be a pact of love whose essential value will be determined by the young blood of Puerto Rico.
Nationalism will not appeal to the cult of the dead to guide the living; Nationalism, depositary of the faith of the Liberators of América.

El Mundo, April 15, 1935, p. 14.
