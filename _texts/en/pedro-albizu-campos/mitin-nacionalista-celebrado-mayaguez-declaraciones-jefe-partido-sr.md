"In official history texts our history before 1898 was a chain of hurricanes and piracy. We are a civilized nationality, a vanguard nationality in the New World. THE NATIONALIST RALLY HELD IN MAYAGUEZ

STATEMENTS BY THE HEAD OF THE PARTY,
MR. ALBIZU CAMPOS 62

Upon being announced, Mr. Albizu Campos, the band performed "La Borinqueña" and a long ovation occurred. Such was the anxiety that was manifested in everyone to hear the word of the illustrious leader of nationalism. He analyzed the system by which the ruling regime carries out the demolition of Puerto Rican society with the fomentation of deep divisions; first: The promotion of women's work in competition with men to cheapen labor and bring about the split of families, making women rivals of men in the economic terrain and therefore disengaging the home. He said that in itself the independence of women was praiseworthy if it was stimulated in good faith, but not if, as in the current circumstances, it is imposed as a means to demoralize a society. He explained how the United States and England are the only countries in which militant feminism exists as a safeguard of the rights of women who are thrown into economic activity. Already in Puerto Rico there are many families where women provide the sustenance of the home, an inversion of roles that is a certain index of the definitive ruin of a people.
Second: Division in financial matters, which exists in the United States but which has been deepened here by the misnamed Socialist Party. In the United States a small group or ruling class exploits almost the entire country; there does not exist there a society or a nation in the correct sense of the word but a large conglomerate that suffers the oppression of the meager oligarchic class that is not concerned about the fate or much less the common aggrandizement of the mass of the country. In Puerto Rico, on the contrary, there was homogeneity among all the components and a great social sense interested in reciprocal help for the perpetuity and conservation of the nation, this is a deep-rooted and unanimous sentiment of Homeland. Consequently, juxtaposing in a class struggle the man who owns nothing against the one who has two pesetas was imposing a strange element of discord that was superfluous in our midst. Third: The division fostered between the white and African races, which is evident by comparing the systems used by the invader in Cuba and Puerto Rico respectively at the termination of the Spanish-American war. In our country, where there was a majority of whites, they imposed as leader the colored class with the triumph of the Republican Party, whose leader was Dr. Barbosa. In Cuba, where the element of color predominated, they raised in the shadow of power a political leadership of individuals of European race and discharged the liberating army composed mostly of men of color.
Fourth: To the above is added the division in the spiritual terrain tending to counteract the undoubted hegemony of Catholicism through the elevation to power of individuals of all Protestant sects, and although nationalism extends its arms to the proselytes of all creeds, it cannot fail to point out the historical fact and the intention and the political meaning that in itself embodies the stimulus offered to the forces contrary to the religious unity of the country.
By way of prologue and epilogue, he expanded on brilliant considerations on all the political and economic problems of Puerto Rico. He referred, for example, to the exchange of currency demonstrating how ab initio property had been devalued by forty percent through the change of the peso for the dollar at the rate of sixty percent when in reality both currencies had equal purchasing power. This collective and rapid depreciation of property together with the cyclone of San Ciriaco, opened the door to latifundismo in the hands of foreigners, a plague so voracious that not even the effort of Muñoz Rivera managed to contain it with his attempt to create in front of the native latifundista, because the latter, as soon as he prospered somewhat, was also entangled in the horrible jaws of crematistic combinations.
He then attacked the large corporations as the cause of the general decadence and even of the ruin of the treasury; the organization of public health externalizing his opinion that "the increase in diseases in the last three decades is due in large part to the terrible quality of the articles offered for consumption, especially canned foods"; public instruction, committed to educating hungry toddlers to whom it would be better to physically sustain with the luxurious budget destined to the branch; and finally to the projects of industrialization of the country, impossible in a colony without tariff protection.
Mr. Albizu Campos concluded his pithy and eloquent dissertation exuberant in a multitude of resounding data said with astonishing precision and opportunity and difficult to reconstruct, exhorting the people of Mayagüez to coldly investigate the capital problems of their Homeland, so that the healthy reaction would operate within their bosom that would vindicate for the Sultana of the West its timbre of honor and its place of honor that it always occupied in the past at the vanguard of our civilizing and liberating struggles. Thunderous applause and expressions of general approval exteriorized the feelings of the public that listened with profound attention for more than two hours to the only political leader who dissects before the people, converting the tribune into a chair, the most intricate national problems to fundamentally study the roots of our prostration.

THE CONFERENCE OF ALBIZU CAMPOS AT THE
UNIVERSITY
ON PUBLIC INSTRUCTION IN PUERTO RICO.
THESIS THAT HE DEVELOPED 63

The conference of Mr. Albizu Campos, dictated on Monday, March 30 under the auspices of the "Cultural Society" of the College of Laws on the throbbing theme of Public Instruction in Puerto Rico, resulted in an act of great cultural significance and of lasting spiritual resonances among the thousands of listeners who heard him at the University or by radio on the island.
The spacious assembly hall was materially occupied by a very select audience; so much was the public that people had to group in the corridors or locate themselves in the windows of the premises.
After brief words introduced by the student José C. Aponte, President of the "Cultural Society", the speaker advanced to the proscenium while the audience burst into a clamorous ovation that lasted for several minutes. On the face of the speaker, the emotional effect of those applauses so warm and spontaneous was noticed.
"I, who have faced hostile audiences so many times," began by saying Mr. Albizu Campos, "can say that this greeting so generous and spontaneous is to move the most battle-hardened man.
"With instruction, as the young Aponte has said, a people is raised or buried; everything depends on the orientation that is imprinted on the educational system. Education is the direct way that the State has to educate its citizens, but in Puerto Rico the State does not exist because it is intervened by the military occupation of the United States.
"Education in the religious state is inspired by the religious principles that inform that country, education in the monarchies is inspired by the monarchical principles, and education in the republics is inspired by the republican principles. The preservation of past values is conditioned by the perspective of posterity. In Puerto Rico, education is not inspired by any defined principle nor does it have a determined orientation because the State does not exist, which is the determining and guiding factor of the educational system,"
The speaker then analyzed education during Spanish rule. Education for that time was Catholic and monarchical, because such were the religion and the regime in force in Spain, although there were in the country prominent personalities who were of anti-Catholic and republican ideology. That education was inspired by the Greco-Latin sources transmuted by the genius of Spain; therefore we are direct heirs of the only culture that exists in the West. Our revolutionaries aspired to constitute a republican state with a heterodox education, but "in my opinion in this particular they were wrong because every nationality must be founded on the religious spirit that animates its inhabitants."
The partisans of republican ideas in Puerto Rico and in Spain to combat the regime and the education in force in the Mother Patria presented the United States as the model nation where the institutions had reached their legal perfection. That false concept disoriented the opinion of our directors and gave the United States a personality that it did not have, to the extreme that the invasion of its troops was received as a blessing for our nationality. The invasion ignored the State and the personality that Puerto Rico already had in the year 1898."
The first thing that the government of the United States did was to seize the educational system, because "education is the easiest instrument to destroy a people", and converted the Commissioner of Instruction, who is a mandatory of the Department of War, into "arbitrator of the cultural destinies of this people." "The current Commissioner of Instruction has omnimodous faculties to make the courses of studies and determine what is to be taught to children. In France instruction is regulated by legislation to that effect."
"The education in force in Puerto Rico," said the speaker, "is informed by a materialistic criterion, is divorced from the Greco-Latin sources and above all educates our youth for submission to an empire and to abide by what that empire does in our Homeland."
The speaker harshly attacked bilingual teaching affirming that it caused "a waste of mental energies and a lack of knowledge of one's own language and of the invading language. No one can penetrate into the idiomatic secret of a foreign language until they know their vernacular language in depth."
The speaker then entered into a detailed analysis of North American education setting forth the following thesis: in the United States there exists an education for the ruling class and another education for the class that is to be dominated.
The education for the ruling class is imparted as is de rigueur in the vernacular language ​​of the nation, is informed by a religious criterion and imposes on the student the most severe moral disciplines. The teaching of the Greco-Latin sources is rigorous: Latin and Greek begin to be taught from the fifth grade. There is the separation of the sexes in these schools destined to educate the future directors of the nation. "They intensify the classical studies because they know that if they disengage from the original sources of Western culture they run the risk of reversing to barbarism in a few generations."

When referring to the bisexual school, the speaker declared himself refractory to it, stating that its establishment obeyed economic and not pedagogical reasons. He said that although women and men can come to know the superior notions of wisdom and scientific knowledge they needed a different methodology that was in consort with the specific mentality of each. There are subjects that women assimilate more easily and there are other subjects that men assimilate more easily; therefore the separation of the sexes and the differentiation of the methods is rigorous. Most of the current texts are written by men for male mentalities and it is necessary that the texts for the female sex be written by women who know how to provoke the desired reaction in the female mentality. "In addition, women should be educated in the isolation of their sex, divorced from any unhealthy suggestion that may disturb their mentality." This pedagogical theory sustained by Mr. Albizu Campos was the most brilliant and original of his learned conference. In this type of education for the class that will rule the national destinies an intense patriotism is sown in the spirit of the student and the criterion is inculcated that his people is the superior of the planet. The teaching is transmitted entirely by male professors.

The education for the masses destined for exploitation "is the package of culture." The bisexual school is used for economy, teaching is disengaged from the classical Greco-Latin sources to keep the people in ignorance and its purpose is to create micro specialization or a cheap professional pseudo-intellectual insensitive to all those values ​​alien to his profession.
"This package of education is what has been implanted in Puerto Rico. Why have the partisans of submission not imported the superior education that exists there on the continent for the ruling classes?

The speaker alluded directly to some statements of the Commissioner of Education, Dr. Padín, before the "Union Church" of this capital. Dr. Padín admits that the educational system does not know where it is going, that in it politics is placed before pedagogy and that it is the Congress of the United States that is authorized to determine the orientation to follow. Dr. Padín delegates the orientation of a people to the invading power and in that he is mistaken. That initiative is exclusively the patrimony of the Puerto Rican nation that is called to determine what its political and cultural destiny should be. And I want to repeat here to save my historical responsibility that a teaching staff, a cloister and a regime that inculcates submission to children is rotten."

"Some time ago I was talking with a pedagogue from this University and I asked him his opinion on the educational problem of Puerto Rico. He told me that pedagogues had no formed opinion on this problem and that as for bilingual teaching it would be necessary to experiment for some more years. And I reply: who gives anyone the right to experiment with the spiritual life of a people?
"A people can resist the physical dispossession of its lands because the land is not destroyed and these pass intact to posterity, but a people cannot resist the moral dispossession perpetrated through the school without being reduced to a herd of slaves that deny even their own personality.
"If this educational system continues we will end in ignorance, which is the worst asylum to which a people can be condemned."
The speaker underlined the absence of civics in the school where on the contrary "the child learns to love a Homeland that is not his and to deny his origins." "According to the "History of Puerto Rico" that is taught in the schools, civilization began in 1898 when the American troops arrived."
The speaker then referred to the University and stated that its directors had an immense privilege and it was to make that teaching institution the true University of Puerto Rico, where teaching was carried out in the vernacular language and classical studies were implanted.
He formulated bitter criticisms against the College of Laws in the sense that jurisprudence was taught on the basis of the opinions of the judges of the United States and not on the basis of our codes, which are the crystallization of a long experience and wisdom. "The United States is sixteen centuries behind us in legal matters," affirmed the speaker, and made an extensive analysis on this topic to verify his thesis with North American authorities.
He also recommended that professors should be hired by opposition, as the students had recommended in a memorial presented to the new Rector.
He refuted the thesis that education is democratic in Puerto Rico because those on whom the tax burden of the country falls cannot send their children to school.
The speaker closed his conference addressing the religious institutions of the country, "I accuse the religious institutions of the country of crossing their arms before the serious crisis that overwhelms the Homeland and for not establishing schools that neutralize the demolishing work of the current educational system," he said.
Mr. Albizu Campos received clamorous ovations in the course of his conference. The act ended around eleven o'clock at night within the greatest enthusiasm.

ON THE MARGIN OF THE POLITICAL MOMENT 64

In front of the nationalistic emancipatory movement there exists in the country the governmental group that has been providing all its cooperation to the prevailing military regime to undermine the foundations of the Homeland.
That governmental group is divided into enemy factions among themselves that dispute with acrimony the public positions that the invader puts at their disposal so that they are instruments of the imperial policy that pretends to annihilate the people of Puerto Rico.
The governmental factions are called unionist, socialist, pure and aliancista. They unite among themselves and then separate to embrace with the opposites according to the calculations that their respective directors formulate in their purpose of assault on the budget.
A shameful spectacle that has placed them in the renunciation of every collective right that has been within their reach and their policy has been of deliberate cooperation with all the purposes of Washington to definitively concentrate all the powers in the imperial military mandatory, Mr. Colonel Roosevelt.
The regime, of course, has always been absolute, irresponsible, as is its military nature. However, the comedy of a legislature and municipal organisms electable by the people has been maintained.
There is no legislative power in Puerto Rico. This truth can be verified by any student of the military regulation with which it is intended to govern the country, called the Organic Charter, imposed on our nationality by the Congress of the United States.
In that regulation it is expressly provided that the imperial legislature will have the power of initiative to create, destroy or reform municipalities.
