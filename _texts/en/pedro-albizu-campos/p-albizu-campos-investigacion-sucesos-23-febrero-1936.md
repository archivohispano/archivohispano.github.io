Public instruction used in the United States to enslave the masses; the foolish arrogance of pretending to guide in the spiritual order a nation whose soul has been forged in pure Christianity; those are our most serious grievances.

The United States of America stands face to face in Puerto Rico with the spirit of Lexington, of Zaragoza, of Ayacucho.

The present imperial policy by which it is intended to dissolve nationalism by terror and assassination is a provocation and an imperialist foolishness to satisfy a few North American corporations.

The people of the United States, if they have not become totally insensitive to the principles that allowed them to be a free nation, must have the common sense, must be guided exclusively by their national interest.

That national interest is guaranteed by respecting the independence of Puerto Rico.

Those are the aspirations of the nationalism of Puerto Rico.
