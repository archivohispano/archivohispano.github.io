Proclamation
on the anniversary of the
Lares revolution: 1935
To Lares, to the hallowed land of the Homeland, which guards the sacred ashes of the founders of the Republic, the nation comes to pay homage and to receive the inspiration of the valor and the sacrifice that the heroes of the Revolution embodied.

The independence of the Homeland and its personality as a free and sovereign nation was written with the noblest blood of all generations.

The National Pilgrimage goes to Lares to rescue the will and the valor that we must emulate to restore the Republic.

September 23rd is the day of the heroes and martyrs of independence. Every Puerto Rican will vibrate before the evocation of the heroic anniversary, will feel free of foreign interventions, and will renew the vow to uproot from the Homeland soil the enemy power that attempts to hold national sovereignty.

Lares is the consecration of the nation to armed revolution, the fixed trajectory of all free peoples.

Present generations must write their heroic page in the history of the Homeland. The spirits of the liberators rise in Lares to offer us freedom and the sword to reconquer it.

Given in San Juan de Puerto Rico, on the nineteenth day of September of 1935, the sixty-eighth year of the proclamation of the Republic.

Nationalist Party of Puerto Rico
The National Board:
P. ALBIZU CAMPOS
President
LUIS F. VELÁZQUEZ
General Secretary

Concept of the Race
By Pedro Albizu Campos
When the President of the Republic of La Plata, Don Hipólito Yrigoyen, instituted the Day of the Race, to what race was the high Argentine magistrate referring? Was he cornering his concept, with a rancid predilection, from the rest of the great ethnic mosaic of America? The Argentine president was referring to the Ibero-American race.

For us, race has nothing to do with biology. Neither moonlit complexion, nor curled hair, nor obliquity of eye. Race is a perpetuity of virtues and characteristic institutions. We are distinguished by our culture, by our valor, by our hidalguía, by our Catholic sense of civilization. Upon the realization of Spanish unity in the union of the Aragonese and Castilian kingdoms and the conquest of independence with the expulsion of the Saracens, Spain, full of herself, launched herself into the epic of the Discovery, the greatest epic of contemporaneity. The Discovery of man by man. God knows what moral cataclysm made possible the separation of humanity to the degree of not recognizing itself! With the Discovery, Spain conquered human unity, gave the world its geographic unity, made the earth a sphere. Man did not know where he lived. His plane of residence was a conjecture. Spain gave him the security of stability and upon feeling firm, man began to walk again. The discovery opened the path for all contemporary investigation in the field of philosophical and natural sciences. Not only did it embody the science of oceanic navigation into a system, but it also brought to man the awareness of the cosmos, set man to navigate in an immense fleet of constellations through infinite space. Will the cosmos be infinite in the eternal perspective of that infinite space or will its destiny be a perpetual crowding and uncrowding in the miracle of creation?
b Palabra, October 19, 1935, p. 7

The primacy of the Spanish race in Europe proved the benefit that a balance of valor and sanctity can give to the world. There is a historical experience that destines us to seek ourselves to return balance to the world. Today the racial sense resurges victoriously. The yellows gather in the Orient to save themselves. Japan feels providentially destined to remake the yellow unity in Asia. The Ethiopian races react equally in Africa. The Anglo-Saxons gather quickly against Latinity. It is the secret law of North American politics to follow the inertia of the politics of its mother country, Great Britain. All have their spiritual weapons from us. Because we were the ones who, by blood and fire, with the cross of the sword and with the sword of the cross, gave to old Europe and to virgin America the tradition of virtue, of bravery, of honor, of sacrifice, of contempt for death and for material goods, which make our race today the only hope of the world.

In these moments in which Europe trembles before the perspective of a warlike catastrophe, the peace of the Universe rests in the hands of two men of our race. Full faith in the values of our race, the world has placed its fate in the hands of two men of ours. Don Salvador de Madariaga y Linazo, of Spain and Argentina, respectively, face, cold, serene, straight in responsibility, the problem of peace, the ideal of our race hoisted on the banner of Christianity with which our friars brought civilization to half the planet and our heroes went around the world.

What dangers are there? For our Race, danger has never existed.

Mourning of the victims of the
Río Piedras Massacre
On a promontory of humid earth, they bid farewell to the mourning, pronouncing eloquent speeches Mr. Ermelindo Santiago, who spoke on behalf of the patriotic Lodge, to which Mr. Ramón S. Pagán belonged; Carrera, as president of the National Federation of Students, and the President of the Nationalist Party, Lawyer Pedro Albizu Campos.

Mr. Albizu Campos said, among other things, that there "represented Puerto Rico entire, from Cabo Rojo to Vieques; from west to east and from north to south, all sharing the immense mourning of freedom" was found. He then said that adhesions of diverse nations to the mourning had already been received; that "the Yankee enemy wants the blood of the Puerto Ricans to run"; that "today they will use one side to realize their purposes and tomorrow they will use another." "From the tomb of freedom," he said, "I ask that Benigno Fernández García, the Attorney General, bring us the accusations against those policemen who assassinated Pagán and his companions, and assassinated José Santiago, when he had already surrendered, when he was unarmed." He said that Colonel Riggs sent the uniformed forces to Río Piedras "with the deliberate purpose of assassinating the nationalist representation of Puerto Rico." He accused in the same manner Governor Winship and Chancellor Chardón of "taking advantage of the student body."
