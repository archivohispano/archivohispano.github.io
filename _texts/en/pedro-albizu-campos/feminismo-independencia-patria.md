FEMINISM AND HOMELAND INDEPENDENCE

By Pedro Albizu Campos

Facing a people anxious for its independence and willing to make
the necessary sacrifices to obtain it, a group of leaders had been placed
unwilling to break with the prevailing regime of the
colony.
When we say a people, of course, we refer to the
Puerto Rican national unity, without distinctions of any kind, and much
less, the absurd distinction, of dividing it between men and women.
The contemporary Puerto Rican woman has suffered, until now,
the abrogation of her representation made by a group of ladies,
who with the title of feminists, have always spoken in her name, and
have fought for the right to suffrage to be granted to her.
If the men who direct public opinion wasted a large
part of their time fighting each other, instead of facing
the invader, the feminists dedicated all their energies to the
acquisition of suffrage, believing without a doubt, that by fighting the
men who opposed this measure, they fulfilled the purpose of
the existence of their organization.
They have obtained suffrage. We are obliged to ask them what
they want it for. Is it perhaps to reinforce colonialism over their Homeland? Or to
annex it to the United States definitively, nullifying its
international personality to turn it into a North American province. ("State" sic.?) Or is it to make Puerto Rico a
Homeland, free, sovereign and independent?
We repeat the public interrogation that before the Nationalist Assembly
we formulated last: Are women going to repeat the sin
of men of dividing among themselves on the vital issue of the
independence of their Homeland?
If feminist women are to present themselves to the country representing,
not a solid libertarian movement, but the various
existing political parties, fighting each other, and disseminating before the
public opinion of the country all the political errors committed by
the leading men during the last thirty years, it is necessary that they
be called to attention in the most energetic way about the
historical responsibility they acquire when exercising suffrage for the first time.
If one wants to give an opinion in politics, one must assume the responsibilities
necessary. The woman as director of political opinion, since in
that ground she has placed herself, has and must expect, that responsibility is demanded of her for what she says.
It is not a patriotic ideal to preach the annexation of one's country to any
other country. Nor is it a patriotic ideal to defend the colonialism that
weighs on the country.

The only transcendental civic duty that one who was born in a
colony has, whether man or woman, is to redeem it from colonialism.
Are the feminist ladies ready to fulfill that
unavoidable duty?

It seems so. We have read with great interest the manifestations
that in the section called "Conversing with the main
feminists of the country", regularly published in the Sunday edition of
EL MUNDO, by Mrs. Angela Negrón Muñoz.

With rare exceptions, the interviews published in that section
with that epigraph, have discouraged us enormously. We have seen
repeated almost all the errors of political, social, economic
and religious appreciation that have ruined a healthy orientation of our
people. Everything, almost everything, has been an accumulation of repetitions of things
old, worn out and refuted, ad nauseam.

The sad case occurs that when the direction headed by
men in the different political parties begins to orient themselves towards
patriotic and definitive purposes, it is from this feminist section that
the most disorienting affirmations rain down on our people.
We insist that the woman, like the man, has to be responsible
for what she says.

Doña Angela confesses to be mortified by certain manifestations
that we made before the last assembly of the party on the occasion of the
exciting offer made to me by the Club de Damas de Puerto Plata,
Dominican Republic, representing the Dominican woman, to
defend the independence of Puerto Rico.

Hasn't Doña Angela been moved by this offer? Haven't
the feminist ladies that she claims feel hurt by
my manifestations before the Assembly been moved? Why, if they represent the highest values of our woman, have they not yet met to send
a message of gratitude to the Club de Damas de Puerto Plata that in such a generous and spontaneous way has offered its assistance to liberate them
from this shameful tutelage?

We owe Doña Angela a congratulation. For the first time, since
we read her, we have seen her invoke the name of immortal women
of our Homeland who sacrificed everything for its independence.

We also owe her a rectification. We should not compare with
figures anointed by the sacrifice for the redemption of this land to
people who maintain as an ideal the annexation of their country to any
other, or defend the empire in their Homeland, imposed by a foreign power. Madam, allow me to tell you that there are impossible parallels.
That the one who writes has a historical responsibility and cannot make
a confusion of values.

Several times we have repeated that we have to go back to the year
1868. At the time of the Grito de Lares. There existed then an
authentic and patriotic leadership in our Homeland, of men and women
willing to make any sacrifice to impose its independence.
We will thank you to continue presenting only before
our people those luminous libertarian figures.
If that was so in '68, as you well say, that more civism was needed than today to defend the cause of freedom, without a doubt,
you will consider it justified, that today, according to what you affirm that it costs
nothing to do the same, we urge men and women to comply with the
most important civic duty: To immediately constitute the free, sovereign and independent Republic.
