Sandino went to Managua because his life and his liberty

"Washington - February 22 - Officials of the
Department of State have stated that they will begin immediately to
investigate the causes of Sandino's murder. This crime was a
brutal action and a violation of the guarantees offered by the
Department of State of the United States to General San-
dino". (El Imparcial, February 23, 1934).
When the civilized world awaited the official declaration of the
North American government clarifying its responsibility for that
horrendous crime, the same North American journalistic agencies
report, dated February 24 of this year, that:
"Nothing indicates that official or public mourning will be declared on
the occasion of the death of General Sandino and so far no
arrest has been made, the murderers are unknown, and it is feared that
they will never be discovered". (El Mundo, February 24, 1934).
We clarify that the so-called National Guard that assassinated the
great patriot is a mercenary military body organized by the
government of the United States under the command of North American
officers, upon whom falls forcibly all the weight of this enormous
responsibility. It should not be difficult to know which officers
were involved in this crime.
It is the height of audacity to want to cover up this vandalic act with
silence because the Liberator Sandino was the living heart of
Our America, which demands a clear explanation of what happened. The
government of the United States has the floor.
As a sacred admonition to prevent the repetition of
barbaric acts that violate all civilized laws and customs
in peace and in war, we declare that the imposition of
gangster methods in international struggles will bring with
it the most bloody reprisals that history knows.
The Homeland is covered in mourning at the passing of the heroic and
generous ally of the independence of Puerto Rico.
The national flag will be flown at half-mast for nine consecutive days.

Letter to
Rafael Martínez Nadal
March 1, 1934
Hon. Rafael Martínez Nadal,
President of the Insular Senate.
San Juan, Puerto Rico.
My dear sir:
This communication is motivated by the national convenience that
the representation to which the Nationalist party is entitled in the
Insular Electoral Board be respected.
The electoral law has been a means of tyranny that the intervening
regime has wielded against the nation through the political
parties that have sponsored its destructive policy. It has always
been manipulated to ensure the triumph of the governmental
faction most loyal and submissive at certain moments. Of course,
everything possible has been done to impede the development of
Nationalism, which is the only opposition movement against the
North American intervention. In the present legalistic electoral state,
only the Republican Union and the Liberal party will have voice and vote
in the Insular Electoral Board, the Socialists only voice, and the Nationalist Party was eliminated.
All the governing parties, Union Republicans, Liberals and
Socialists, are against the present legalistic electoral state,
for one reason or another, and their directors have spoken of the need
to create an electoral law that gives guarantees to all
sectors of opinion.
We have seen with great astonishment that all that preaching is reduced
to granting voice and vote to the Socialist party in the Insular Electoral
Board, with which we agree, of course, because
that is a right of which it would be a gross immorality to deprive the
aforementioned political group.
El Mundo, March 2, 1934, p. 1 and 13; and El Imparcial, March 3, 1934, p. 12

To Nationalism, however, it is intended to completely dispossess it
of all representation in the Insular Electoral Board.
Now, every electoral law is made to give guarantees to the
opposition to the government. The governing parties do not need
guarantees, because power is not limited to giving them guarantees, but
it grants them all the privileges to keep them strong, because
they are the instruments of its policy. Thus the Republican Union, the
Liberal party and the Socialist party, as long as they are as they are,
governing parties, the regime will ensure their development by all
the means at its disposal, which are all, in a tyrannical
foreign intervention such as the one that wants to hold our sovereignty.
Moreover, to distribute the budget among those parties, -
of course, excluding the positions of high remuneration of which
the Yankee power disposes at its whim-, elections are not needed.
This observation is so true that the Yankee power does not
take into consideration the electoral result when it divides the
budget among the governing parties. And it should not be forgotten
that all the interference of those parties in the present regime is
limited to the participation of their followers in the subordinate positions
of the budget, to deliver, in the sacred name of Puerto Rico,
all the vital interests of the Homeland to its intervening enemies.
The parties called of government in this regime, by virtue
of the same, are impotent to do anything affirmative in defense
of Puerto Rico. If they wanted to take any initiative, they would forcibly
stumble with the intervening regime, which would deny them the budgetary good
and would automatically condemn them to extinction. The
same insular legislature has served to verify its uselessness
to defend the patriotic interests before the absolute veto that weighs
on all its legislation.
We do not want to discuss the grave responsibility in which
one incurs when constituting a "governing" party under the
foreign military tyranny, like the one that reigns here. But we must
call attention to the fact that there is no right to continue serving as
a dupe to the Yankee power to obstruct the development of
Nationalism, which represents the militant claim to the absolute
independence of the Homeland; that it be the sole arbiter of its destinies
in all orders of its national life.

We will be told that one wants to work furtively in favor of the
patriotic interests in the same enemy camps. That tactic is as
ineffective as has been proven in the thirty-five long and
terrible years of Yankee and demoralizing intervention, because
it tends to weaken the virile fiber of nationality.
We appear through your mediation before the legislative body
that you have the honor of presiding, so that the blemish that weighs on the history of the legislature for having
lent itself to the systematic obstruction of nationalism, which is the
only cause that is ours in absolute as Puerto Ricans, is not repeated.
The Nationalist Party, despite all the arbitrariness
of the regime, has maintained in the polls, in all the elections,
from 1920 onwards, the protest against foreign interference
in the affairs of Puerto Rico, maintaining unblemished its right to
absolute independence. Currently its ideology informs all
public opinion. As the only opposition party, it is the most
called to have representation in the Insular Electoral Board.
I avail myself of the opportunity to express to the Mr. President
of the Insular Senate the most distinguished consideration.
S.S.S.,
P. ALBIZU CAMPOS
President of the
Nationalist Party
