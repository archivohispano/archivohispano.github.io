I¿res Revolution: 1935
To Lares, to the sacred land of the Homeland, which guards the sacred ashes of the founders of the Republic, the nation converges to render homage and to receive the inspiration of the valor and sacrifice that the heroes of the Revolution embodied.
The independence of the Homeland and its personality as a free and sovereign nation was written with the noblest blood of all generations.
The National Pilgrimage is directed to Lares to rescue the will and the valor that we must emulate to restore the Republic.
September 23 is the day of the heroes and martyrs of independence. Every Puerto Rican will vibrate, before the evocation of the heroic anniversary, will feel free from foreign interventions, and will renew the vow to uproot from the homeland the enemy power that intends to hold national sovereignty.
Lares is the consecration of the nation to the armed revolution, the fixed trajectory of all free peoples.
Present generations must write their heroic page in the history of the Homeland. The manes of the liberators rise in Lares to offer us liberty and the sword to reconquer it.
Given in San Juan de Puerto Rico, on the nineteenth day of September of 1935, sixty-eighth year of the proclamation of the Republic.

Concept of the Race
By Pedro Albizu Campos
When the President of the Republic of Plata, Don Hipólito
Yrigoyen, instituted the Day of the Race, to what race was the high
Argentine magistrate referring? Was he cornering his concept, with an old
preference, the rest of the great ethnic mosaic of America? The Argentine president was referring to the Ibero-American race.
For us, race has nothing to do with biology. Neither moonlit complexion, nor kinky hair, nor obliquity of the eye. Race is a perpetuity of virtues and characteristic institutions. We distinguish ourselves by our culture, by our valor, by our nobility, by our Catholic sense of civilization. When Spanish unity was realized in the union of the Aragonese and Castilian kingdoms and independence was conquered with the expulsion of the Saracens, Spain, full of herself, launched herself into the epic of the Discovery, the greatest epic of contemporaneity. The Discovery of man by man. God knows what moral cataclysm made possible the separation of humanity to the point of not recognizing itself! With the Discovery, Spain conquered human unity, gave the world its geographic unity, made the earth a sphere. Man did not know where he lived. His residence plan was a conjecture. Spain gave him the security of stability and upon feeling firm, man began to walk again. The discovery opened the path for all contemporary research in the field of philosophical and natural sciences. Not only did it shape the science of oceanic navigation into a system, but it brought to man the awareness of the cosmos, put man to navigate in an immense fleet of constellations through infinite space. Will the cosmos be infinite in the eternal perspective of that infinite space or will its destiny be a perpetual crowding and uncramping in the miracle of creation?
b Palabra, October 19, 1935, p. 7

The primacy of the Spanish race in Europe proved the benefit that a balance of valor and sanctity can give to the world. There is a historical experience that destines us to seek ourselves in order to restore balance to the world. Today the racial sense resurges victorious. The yellows group together in the East to save themselves. Japan feels providentially destined to remake the yellow unity in Asia. The Ethiopian races react equally in Africa. The Anglo-Saxons quickly reunite against Latinity. It is a secret law of North American politics to follow the inertia of the politics of its mother country, Great Britain. They all have their spiritual weapons from us. Because we were the ones who, by blood and fire, with the cross of the sword and with the sword of the cross, gave to old Europe and virgin America the tradition of virtue, of valor, of honor, of sacrifice, of contempt for death and material goods, which make our race today the only hope of the world.
In these moments in which Europe trembles before the
perspective of a warlike catastrophe, in the hands of two men
of our race rests the peace of the Universe. Full faith in the values
of our race the world has placed its fate in the hands of two
men of ours. Don Salvador de Madariaga y Linazo of Spain
and Argentina, respectively, face, cold, serene,
straight of responsibility, with the problem of peace, the ideal of
our race hoisted in the flag of Christianity with which our
friars brought civilization to half the planet and our heroes
went around the world.
What are there dangers? For our Race danger has never
existed.

Mr. Albizu Campos said, among other things, that Puerto Rico as a whole was "represented there, from Cabo Rojo
to Vieques; from west to east and from north to south, all sharing
the immense mourning of freedom". Then he said that adhesions had already been received from various nations to the mourning; that "the Yankee enemy wants the blood of Puerto Ricans to run"; that "today they will use one faction to carry out their purposes and tomorrow they will use another".
