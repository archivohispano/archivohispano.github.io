Sixty thousand dollars bail was posted yesterday before the
Federal Commissioner, attorney Ramón C. Juliá, by Mr.
Pedro Albizu Campos, president of the Nationalist Party of
Puerto Rico; Juan Antonio Corretjer, director of the nationalist
newspaper La Palabra and General Secretary of the collec-
tive; Luis F. Velázquez, Treasurer of the party; Clemente Soto
Vélez, director of the nationalist weekly "Armas" which is
published in Caguas; Erasmo Velázquez, Secretary of the
Nationalist Municipal Board of Caguas; and Juan Juarbe Juarbe,
Private Secretary of Mr. Albizu Campos. Each one
posted bail in the sum of ten thousand dollars. The guarantors of
the Head of the Nationalist Party were Mr. José Pascual
Berríos and Juan Valdejulli Rodríguez; the guarantors of the director
of La Palabra were Mr. José R. Fuertes and Rufo
Noya; the guarantors of Mr. Juan Juarbe Juarbe, were Mr.
Edmundo Fernández and José G. Cardé. Other gentlemen
posted bail for the rest of the accused nationalists.

Another of the accused is Pablo Marrero Rosado, but yesterday
he had not appeared to answer the accusation.

El Mundo, March 6, 1936, p. 1. The execution of Colonel Riggs precipitated
the repression and persecution against Nationalism. Immediately the regime
initiated a wave of raids on the residences of the most prominen-
te nationalists. On Thursday, March 5, 1936 (ten days after the death of Riggs), the
president of the Nationalist Party, Albizu Campos, together with Juan Antonio Corret-
jer, Luis F. Velázquez, Clemente Soto Vélez, Erasmo Velázquez, Juan Juarbe Juarbe
and Pablo Rosado Ortiz were arrested and accused of an alleged crime of
"conspiracy to overthrow the government of the United States in Puerto Rico by
means of force", before the United States District Court in Puerto Rico,
(called Federal Court).

How the Accusation Reads:

The accusation that motivated the arrest yesterday of the leaders
nationalists mentioned above and for which they posted
bail in the expressed sum in order to remain in liber-
tad provisional reads as follows: "Before me, Ramón C. Juliá, Commis-
sioned of the United States for the district of Puerto Rico, perso-
nally appeared today, March 3, 1936, in San
Juan, Puerto Rico, Donald A. Draughon, marshal of the United
States for the District Court of Puerto Rico, who des-
after being duly sworn, deposes and declares that
about three years ago and especially since October 24 of
1935 in the places of the island of Puerto Rico later rela-
cionados, in said district, Pedro Albizu Campos, of Aguas
Buenas; Juan Antonio Corretjer, Luis F. Velázquez and Pablo
Rosado Marrero of San Juan; Clemente Soto Vélez and Erasmo
Velázquez, of Caguas and Juan Juarbe Juarbe, of Aguas Buenas;
in violation of sections 6, 7 and 88 of the title of the Code of
United States, illegally conspired to overthrow, de-
derribar and destroy by force the Government of the United
States in Puerto Rico, and conspired to recruit soldiers
to engage in armed hostilities against the United States
and open posts for such recruitment, contrary to the provisions
in the statute for the case provided and against the peace and dig-
nity of the United States of America.

The declarant testifies, in addition, that he has reasons to
believe that and so believes."

Among the witnesses that appear at the bottom of the accusation
to sustain the same we were able to read the names of the
insular guards Salvador Más and Juan Pagán Rohena.

The federal Commissioner, Mr. Juliá, refused to give us the
complete list of witnesses stating that this deta-
it had no importance and that also on the day of the trial
many more would be cited that did not appear in the arrest warrant
because it is not customary to indicate all the names of the
witnesses in said document.

A Telephone Call:

Mr. Albizu Campos, principal lawyer in the process
that is being followed in the District Court of San Juan
against the young nationalist Dionisio Pearson, was located tem-
prano in the courtroom when at about 9:20, while
he was chatting with Judge A. R. de Jesús in the office next to the
private office of the aforementioned magistrate, he received a lla-
mada telephone from Marshal Donald A. Draughon, of the Court
Federal, by which he was required to appear
in said court as soon as possible since against him
there was an arrest warrant issued for the alleged crime that
has already been expressed. Mr. Albizu Campos immediately
gathered the other defense lawyers Mr. Gilberto
Concepción de Gracia, Federico E. Virella, Julio Pinto Gandía,
Rafael Ortiz Pacheco and J.M. Toro Nazario to inform them of
the situation that had arisen and also instruct them that mien-
while he left for the Federal Court, they continued the hearing of the case
of Pearson. Accompanied by his private secretary, Mr.
Juarbe, and the lawyers Toro Nazario and Ortiz Pacheco, the
president of the Nationalist party left the Court of
District of San Juan arriving at the Federal Court at about
9:45 in the morning.

Pearson's Trial Is Suspended:

Very few people realized in the Court of Dis-
What was happening to the district. Judge A.R. de Jesús occupied the
presidency to resume the hearing of the Pearson trial but the
lawyer Concepción de Gracia asked that it be suspended
until two in the afternoon in view of the fact that, according to information
and belief, the principal lawyer was arrested in the Court
Federal as well as some of the defense witnesses.
The lawyer Concepción de Gracia informed the
court, that he had received at that moment information
that he believed to be true to the effect that the homes of various
nationalists had been raided by federal agents
known as "G-Men".

Judge A.R. de Jesús "in view of the situation through which
the defense is going," he said, agreed to the request formu-
lada suspending the trial until two in the afternoon at the same time that
gave excuses to the jurors for the "unfortunate coincidences"
that were arising in the hearing of the case and that
obligaban very much to his regret to the delay of the same.

The prosecutor Marcelino Romani neither fought nor agreed to the
motion of suspension, merely limiting himself to saying that his
resolution rested on the discretionary powers of the
Court.

Mr. Albizu Campos then announced that he, ade-

As a first question for the record, Mr. Juliá
asked Mr. Albizu Campos if he in his name and in
name of the rest of the defendants, alleged to be innocent or if
declared guilty.

-"Not guilty"- replied Mr. Albizu Campos.

Snyder and Albizu Campos Speak

A bond of ten thousand dollars was set for each of the
accused the prosecutor Snyder argued in favor of the decision of the
Commissioner Mr. Juliá to agree, declaring that "the
bond imposed was proportionate to the seriousness of the crime that
the accused were charged."

"For us," declared Mr. Albizu Campos, "the
amount of the bond imposed is not important. We are of
opinion that the alleged seriousness of the accusation made against us
should not serve as a basis for setting bail, but the
moral certainty that they have of our appearance before this
court on the date and time that you have to indicate.
We are men of honor, fearless men and
men who know how to face any situation and take
charge of the responsibility of their actions at any time
and in any circumstance. This is a historic case, unique in
the history of the relations of Puerto Rico with the United
States. The Government of the United States through its Depart-
tamento de Justicia is trying to bring by means of this pro-
before this court, the men who present the opposi-
tion in Puerto Rico against the North American regime."

The prosecutor Snyder on hearing these words smiles and makes a
negative gesture with his head, but Mr. Albizu Campos,
seriously continues:

"In view of that fact, these men, including the
president of the Nationalist Party of Puerto Rico, who is
one of the defendants, as well as a lawyer for the other defendants,
wish to declare that for more than a week they have been
receiving absolutely confidential information and from fuen-
inexpugnable reveladoras that it was the purpose deci-
dido of the Government of the United States order these arrests
under any pretext and with the deliberate purpose of pro-
stir up a public disorder in which blood is shed and
these defendants are killed."

The prosecutor Snyder objects to having those words recorded
record those words, but Mr. Albizu Campos interrupts
him saying: "Let me finish and then you make the
declarations you want."

-"Very well, continue"- replies the Prosecutor.

Mr. Albizu Campos continues: "With that information
in our possession and before any official authority of
this court ordered our arrest, this defendant voluntarily-
mente appeared before this court on March 4 at
eight in the morning and asked the prosecutor, Mr. Snyder, if he had
against him any arrest warrant; the prosecutor told us no, that
he did not possess or have any arrest warrant against me; I visited
then the office of the marshal Mr. Draughon of whom
I requested the same information, but Mr. Draughon,
just like the prosecutor Snyder, also declared to me that in his possession
there was no arrest warrant against me; in Mr. Snyder's office
was at that moment his assistant the
prosecutor Jorge Ortiz Toro, accompanying me the lawyer Rafael
Ortiz Pacheco; the prosecutor and I had a conversation in
very cordial tones about vital issues related to
United States and Puerto Rico and it was in the course of this
conversation that I informed him of the reports that we had to
effect that federal agents were trying to kill us at the
first opportunity; the prosecutor assured me in the most
formal that there was no such attempt on the part of the agents
federal and that any personal guarantee that I asked for he
was in a position to give it to me as far as it allowed him
the powers of the court. And to leave I told the prosecutor that if
if those arrest warrants were issued to execute them, they would not
have any difficulty on our part; the
Mr. Draughon, whom I also informed of the attempt of the
federal agents told me to forget about that because he
assured me that none of that would ever be done.

"It was at that moment that I made them know that on them
I discharged all the responsibility of a shedding
of blood and that the death of any member of the Party
Nationalist was most likely to provoke a revolu-
that I placed the responsibility for the authorities of this
Court.

"I also told Mr. Draughon that I was in
conditions to appear before this Court at the first lla-
mada telephone that I was given or the first telegraphic message
graphic that was sent to me in that sense without worrying about the
severity of the accusation that could be imputed to me and that I
could in the same way respond for the other defendants
because I was sure that none of our men
would refuse to assume responsibility for their actions. The nacio-
nalists never shirk the responsibility for their actions. For the
better fulfillment of the duties of its officers, I offered them
that all means would be facilitated by us, por-
because the issue involved in this entire process is not
a personal issue against any of the officers of this
Court, but that there was involved an issue of inter-
national. In compliance with that agreement was that
morning, finding myself in the District Court of San
Juan, where, as is known, is being held the process
the most important criminal record in the history of
our people, I received a phone call to come
immediately to the Court, and accompanied by my secretary
private, Mr. Juarbe, I have appeared before the
authorities of the court."

Mr. Albizu Campos paused in his testimony
to point out several of his arrested companions who were esta-
ban to his side, Messrs. Juan Antonio Corretjer, Luis F.
Velázquez, Clemente Soto Vélez and Erasmo Velázquez,
saying of them that they had also come voluntarily
to the Court.

The director of "La Palabra", Mr. Juan Antonio Corret-

The lawyer Cayetano Coll y Cuchí, who appears as

expressed so: "This is a completely wrong policy
that will do much harm to the United States and much benefit to

Benítez Flores Speaks:

The lawyer Manuel Benítez Flores, member of the
Liberal Central Board, made us the following comment: "Nin-
gún act in Puerto Rico has ever had a significance
more intense or a wider repercussion in America,
especially at this moment in which it is announced by the
President Roosevelt the policy of good neighbor".

Leaving the Court:

Around one in the afternoon Mr. Albizu Campos left
of the Federal Court accompanied by the other defendants and the
friends who came to offer him their services and cooperation.
When the crowd managed to see him at the exit door
broke out in acclamations and cheers to Albizu Campos and the
independence. The head of nationalism began to walk on foot
towards the Hotel Palace, where he is staying provi-
sionally while the Pearson trial is being held.
When the session is adjourned in the afternoon Mr. Albizu
Campos leaves immediately for his home in Aguas Buenas.
When he arrived at the Hotel Palace, in view of the fact that the people who
seguían desired that he explain what had happened to him in the court,
from the lobby of the entrance door he briefly addressed
the public narrating the entire course that his process had been following
before the Federal Court and of his other
companions.

To a Reporter of "El Mundo":

"This arrest means the defeat of the empire of the United
States in Puerto Rico", said Mr. Albizu Campos to a re-
porter of this newspaper who requested a comment from him on the
situation of the moment. And he added: "The
arrest, it remains to confirm the murder. If this is perpetuated,
the revolution will come immediately."

Another Lawyer at His Service:

At about two fifteen in the afternoon, the lawyer
Ernesto Ramos Antonini, "floor" leader of the liberal minority
in the House of Representatives, met with Mr.
Albizu Campos at the Hotel Palace to offer his services
professional in the defense of his cause and of the cause of the
other nationalists arrested yesterday for alleged conspiracy
against the Government of the United States. Mr. Albizu Cam-
pos cordially accepted the offer of cooperation that
Mr. Ramos Antonini made him.

"Serene and Alert!":

A large group of nationalists accompanied Mr.
Albizu Campos along San José street to the Hotel Palace
when yesterday the afternoon session of the Dionisio trial ended
Pearson.

Upon entering the hotel, Mr. Albizu Campos, addressing
to his followers, he told them: "Serene and alert!" Almost all them
raised their hands in greeting and some wanted to
applaud, but Mr. Albizu Campos cut short the attempt
