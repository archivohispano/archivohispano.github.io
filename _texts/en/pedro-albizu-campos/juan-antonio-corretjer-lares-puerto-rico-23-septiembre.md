EIGHTH ASSIGNMENT
The Supreme Court erred in upholding the ruling of the District
Court of San Juan, which held that the case was one of aggravated assault.
The local statute that establishes the crime of assault and
aggravated assault is an exact copy of the same statute of the state of
Texas.
Since we had no precedents in Puerto Rico, we have had
to resort to those Texas statutes.

The Court expressed:
"In order for an assault against a person who is an officer
to constitute aggravated assault, as provided by the Penal Code of
1895, Article 601, the assault must have been committed
against an officer who is fulfilling the legal duties of his
office, etc... It must appear that the person assaulted was an officer
in the actual performance of his duties and that said assault was
carried out as an interruption of his duties."
And in Onne v. State, 132 S.W. 352, 60 Texas, Cr. 440, the Court
expressed:
"In order for an assault against a person who is an officer
to constitute aggravated assault, it must appear that at the time of the assault,
the person assaulted was legally performing the fulfillment of his duties
and that the assault was carried out with the intention of interrupting such duties."

It is clear from the evidence that the accused did not go to
visit Mr. Toro for any matter affecting the official duties
of the latter.
Mr. del Toro himself testified that he was resting,
that he had just finished lunch as usual, that he had
walked after lunch; that he could be seen in his office
for other matters not related to his official duties.
(Rec. p. 6-12).
The Prosecutor could not show a single official function in which
Mr. del Toro was occupied at the time of the alleged
assault.

TENTH ASSIGNMENT
The Supreme Court erred in upholding the ruling of the District
Court of San Juan affirming that the penalty was neither unusual nor
unduly severe.
The accused was deprived of a jury trial in the Court
in that case.

SELECTED WORKS
Light and Shadow

Shadow, dreams, creation, here is the scale of Eternity.
God made everything from Nothing, that is, from the Shadow. From it
He also drew the light for human understanding of the creative secret;
but the best mystical understanding is not possible without the light
extinguishing the retina and restoring the empire of the Shadow.
Shadow is the soul as a divine and immortal spark. In it
the mystery of light and shadow is resolved as in Almighty God.
Twilight, lunar eclipse, solar eclipse, extinction of
stars, disappearance of universes, restitution of the mystical sha-
dow, creative matrix, which God will make luminous in his divine power,
to individualize everything into beings that will always carry with them the
perpetual remembrance of their managing mother, the Shadow.
Bodies fall apart and shadows are perpetuated. Immor-
tality is the eternal law.
God enjoys the mutations in his own work, and to the
elements He governs them with the internal form, that is, they will grow like his
shadow. Morphology is a revelation.
Light as emerged from the arcane, by decree of the Omnipotent,
is like the son: it will always project its own mother, the Shadow.
The initiation is in the understanding of the Shadow. The
shadows will speak to us and reveal to us the great secret; they will sing to
us, and make audible the harmony of their divine concert.
For understanding of the All we will extinguish the suns and the stars. We will re-enter the Genitrix Shadow.

By Pedro Albizu Campos
Río Piedras, February 11, 1935.
La Palabra, December 2, 1915, p2. It was later reproduced in the magazine Criterio,
