The worker has no guarantees of any kind if he lives in the *batey*. He has almost seven months of winter without any income.
In this industry, the harshest imaginable, the workers earned an average of eighty-five cents a day during the last harvest.
The government signed an agreement with the sugar producers reducing wages. The *Central Aguirre* itself has confessed this in a leaflet.
The government and the sugar producers are one and the same. The government has made a pact with itself and wants to impose on the workers the conditions that suit the sugar industry.
It has mobilized the Police to intimidate the strikers. There are more than two hundred policemen in *Guayama*. The carts and trains of cane pass guarded by the public force, because the strike is much feared.
The government is a strikebreaker. The strike that was defunct by the will of the officious ex-leaders of the labor movement, today revived, has had immediate success because to encourage the strikebreakers they are paid twenty percent more than what is stipulated in the official agreement.
Nevertheless, the strike is standing in the south and in the east under the direction of the *Asociación de Trabajadores de Puerto Rico*, which is adding to its bosom all the workers of the nation in a saving movement for the proletariat and for the *Patria*.
What do the strikers ask for? Something very modest. That no one work twelve hours a day anywhere; that it be taken into account that the cost of living has increased, doubling the price of many basic necessities, but the worker, with inexplicable resignation, limits himself to demanding a forty percent increase; that he be paid in money; that the law be obeyed.
When it is said that a worker earns eighty cents, in reality a salary of forty-five cents is fixed when taking into account the decline in the purchasing power of the dollar.
The government wants to impose these enslaving conditions on the workers who represent ninety percent of the population. This is sugar slavery imposed by force. It is up to us to abolish it.
San Juan,
January 18, 1934.

Albizu Campos, powerful and brilliant orator, delivers a speech before the cane workers during the strike of the sugar industry in January 1934.

SELECTED WORKS 21
Proclamation upon the
death of Sandino
The nation learned of the premeditated assassination perpetrated in *Managua*, in the august person of the Liberator General *César Augusto Sandino*, by information transmitted on February twenty-third of this year by the North American news agencies.
Here is a summary of said information:
"*Managua*, February 22 - The brave and heroic General *Augusto Sandino*, who tenaciously and resolutely defended his *Patria* against the soldiers sent to the Republic of *Nicaragua* by *Coolidge* and by *Hoover*, was assassinated today in the streets of *Managua* while passing accompanied by three of his aides.
"For more than three years *Sandino* was pursued by American soldiers and marines, without them managing to capture him, and he did not decide to lay down his arms until the government of the United States withdrew its marines and soldiers from Nicaraguan territory. This incident made *Sandino* a hero of America and left a deep impression of antipathy in *Hispanoamérica* towards the United States.
Having decreed an armistice by the governments of *Nicaragua* and the United States, he appeared in public for the first time today after his long and painful exile. He had not been in *Managua* for two hours when the rifles of the enemies who pursued him through the jungles and mountains, fired upon him. *Sandino* fell dead instantly. His three companions were seriously injured.
*Sandino* went to *Managua* because his life and his freedom had been guaranteed by the Department of State of the United States."
"*Washington* - February 22 - Officials of the Department of State have stated that they will begin immediately to investigate the causes of the assassination of *Sandino*. This crime was a brutal action and a violation of the guarantees offered by the Department of State of the United States to General *Sandino*." (*El Imparcial*, February 23, 1934).
When the civilized world awaited the official declaration of the North American government that would clarify its responsibility for that horrendous crime, the same North American journalistic agencies, inform under date of February 24 of this year that:
"Nothing indicates that official or public mourning will be declared on the occasion of the death of General *Sandino* and so far no arrest has been made, the murderers are unknown and it is feared that they will never be discovered." (*El Mundo*, February 24, 1934).
We clarify that the so-called National Guard that assassinated the great patriot is a mercenary military corps organized by the government of the United States under the command of North American officers, on whom inevitably falls the full weight of this enormous responsibility. It should not be difficult to know which officers were involved in this crime.
It is the height of audacity to want to cover this vandalic act with silence because the Liberator *Sandino* was the living heart of Our America, which demands a clear explanation of what happened. The government of the United States has the floor.
As a sacred admonition to prevent the repetition of barbaric acts that violate all civilized laws and customs in peace and war, we declare that the imposition of gangster methods in international struggles will bring with it the bloodiest reprisals known to history.
The *Patria* is covered in mourning before the transit of the heroic and generous ally of the independence of *Puerto Rico*.
The national flag will be raised at half-mast for nine consecutive days.
