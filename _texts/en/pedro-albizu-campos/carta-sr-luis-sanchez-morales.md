July 1, 1932.

Hon. Luis Sánchez Morales,
President of the Senate and to the
señores Senators.

Señores:
In our memorial lodged before the legislature at the opening of
the present extraordinary session, by virtue of a mandate of the National
Board of the Nationalist Party of Puerto Rico, we reclaimed for
our patriotic organization equality before the electoral law. The Press
of today informs us that that body has approved in third reading the
bill on electoral law submitted to its consideration.
Nationalism was not granted the opportunity to be heard despite
the terms of its already cited memorial. The bill has been approved
unanimously although it does not take into consideration the situation of the
Nationalist party.
This action of the Senate is simultaneous with the letter from the Executive
Secretary communicating to us the suspension of the inscription of the new
candidacies that we have lodged in his secretariat, pretexting the wait
for a new opinion from the attorney general that illustrates him on the
right to inscribe candidacies by convention, when by right already
recognized, the Nationalist party has lodged general candidacies.
We see in all that machinations of the intervening governor to
impede that Nationalism can compete at the polls. What happened in the year 1924 is repeated. It is hard for us to believe that that body without
distinction of parties has entered into an antipatriotic understanding
with the North American power, to avoid that the
nationalist will of the country be manifested peacefully in the electoral contests.
Before the gravity of the situation that that policy could bring if it is
proven to be true, it is our duty to reclaim again, equality
before the electoral law, and, to that effect, we solicit that we be granted to be
heard.

With all consideration
P. ALBIZU CAMPOS
President of the Nationalist Party
