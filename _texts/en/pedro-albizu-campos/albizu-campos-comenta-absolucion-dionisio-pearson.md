With all due respect to the honor of a man, I
state that the policeman Cebollero had no participation in this
horrendous crime. This is demonstrated by the evidence obtained yesterday.
We have been asked about the attitude of the defense
regarding the request of several Jurors to be allowed
to visit their homes with a sub-marshal. The defense, if this
matter had been formally raised before the Court,
would hold the same opinion that it had with the other Juror, that is,
trusting in the honorability of the persons who consti-
tute the present Jury, the defense believes that it is a
convenience in the interest of public justice to allow
the Jury to be under their word of honor in their own homes,
for their rest and tranquility of their most loved ones.

We place all our confidence in the honorability
and equanimity of the Jury. We publicly proposed for this
Jury the same regime of honor that we suggested for the
previous Jury: that is, that in the hours that the
Court is not in session, they would be allowed to retire to their homes
for their rest and tranquility of their most loved ones.

Honor is the fundamental tradition of our race and
has always been maintained with the millenary valor of the
peoples that make up the great Ibero-American family.

The Jury as an institution has a virtue, and that is, its inde-
pendence. In the exercise of that independence, the most
elementary sense of responsibility can never be lacking,
because this is unwavering when it comes to personal liberty,
of individual liberty and of social security. This
Jury with this verdict, and the Court to which it has belonged, have
fulfilled a duty of justice and of patriotism.

The natural national rejoicing provoked by the acquittal of
Don Dionisio Pearson should be received with another supreme
national longing, as a solemn vow before the memory of the
founders of our nationality: that the Porto-
Ricans never be the ones to shed the blood of our brothers;
that no feeling of revenge guide us and that
we maintain Homeland solidarity above all
passion.

El Mundo, March 19, 1936, p. 5.
