I wish to state that they are not permitted to vote on anything pertaining to the Partido Nacionalista and that they cannot hold any position whatsoever in the Partido Nacionalista.

-If you would be so kind, I would like you to explain more definitively the word "we discourage." Are they prohibited from belonging to the Partido?

-I wish to state that we do not try to attract them. If they apply and come in good faith, we accept them.

-And what about the members of the Guardia Nacional?

-We discourage them, as well as those who serve in the Ejército de Estados Unidos.

-Surely you do not encourage them?

-We cannot prevent that if a member of the Guardia Nacional, or of the Ejército de Estados Unidos of the regular army...

-As I understand you, you do not encourage them?

-We discourage them.

-Could you estimate how many members there are in the Partido?

-If you allow me, I will give you the reasons to make things clear. Our criterion is that the political corruption prevalent in Puerto Rico is due to public employment, and that is why we are divided. We consider that the people should not fight for something as dirty or outrageous as the budget. The budget is the source of disturbance between the Gobierno de Estados Unidos and this community, the main source of disturbance. -Señor Albizu, I forgot to ask you. When you had these cards printed, who distributed them to the various municipal boards?

-I ordered the distribution.

-And were the recruits in each municipal board asked to give the information requested on those cards?

-Yes.

-And were they registered in the registry books?

-No, they first had to be members of the Junta Municipal before they could sign this card.

-As I see it, there is no place here for the signature.

-May I explain this card to you? This card cannot be signed by anyone who has not been a nationalist for more than one year from the date of issuance of the card. This card was not issued for people who joined the Partido Nacionalista during the last year.

-But in addition to filling out this card. Did they also not sign one in the manner of an enlistment book?

-I believe that each Junta tried to keep its own record.

-Each Junta Municipal?

-Yes, but the thing consisted of signing the card.

-Could you tell me approximately how many members the Partido Nacionalista has in San Juan?

-In San Juan, around two thousand.

-And in Ponce?

-I believe more than that.

-More than two thousand?

-Yes. It is a rough estimate.

-In Aguas Buenas?

-I believe that the entire community is Nationalist.

-How many are there in the community? I cannot say. But I suppose there are approximately a thousand.

-I suppose then that in all those places all those members of the partido are members of the Ejército Libertador.

-No, because many of them came to the Partido last year.

-I see. How many members joined the Partido Nacionalista during the past year?

-I suppose, this is a quick estimate. I do not like to give exaggerated figures; let us suppose, twenty thousand.

-That means that twenty thousand were members of the partido a year ago.

-Yes.

-Those twenty thousand have been enlisted in the Ejército Libertador.

-Could you, or would you, tell us how you make your living?

-I refuse.

-Do you not receive a salary from the Partido?

-Nothing.

-Does anyone, any officer, receive a salary from the Partido?

-No one.

-Do the other officers dedicate all their time to the Partido?

-No, they cannot. They have to earn a living.

-But you dedicate all your time to it.

-I said practically.

-You said this morning that there is a Junta Municipal in Nueva York. I suppose that the Junta Nacional stays in contact with it, that you maintain correspondence.

-Yes.

-Do you send them copies of all your decrees?

-Yes. In the mail they know all that. All our correspondence is violated. The Gran Jurado should hear the witnesses on that point.

The Gran Jurado, I am sure, would gladly hear any of your witnesses on that point. It will determine in the end whether or not what you have just said is true.

-Returning to Puerto Rico, do you have a nursing corps in the Ejército Libertador?

-We women try to make them nurses. It is not organized, but I have advised the women of the country to try to do something practical for social welfare, to lend assistance to the community and to the partido Nacionalista, but it is very difficult to organize women.

-But, do you have a plan that you have tried to put into practice to organize a nursing corps?

-We hope to make every woman in Puerto Rico a nurse.
