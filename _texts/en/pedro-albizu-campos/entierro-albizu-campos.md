Returning in the afternoon, he informed a comrade of ours that Chief Castilla, in conjunction with Deputy Marshal Bird, of the Federal Court, had gone to the house of Mr. Albizu Campos with a warrant, but that they were unable to execute it because his wife, Doctor Laura Meneses de Albizu, after examining the warrant, noted that there was an error in the name of the street, as it seems the warrant indicated the house to be searched as being on "Huertas Abajo" street, and the residence in the town of Mr. Albizu Campos is on "Carmen" street. So that the agents of the law would realize that in reality the warrant was defective in that extreme, a group of nationalists who were in the vicinity of the residence, led them to the corner so that they could see there the sign on which the true name of the street appeared written. Mr. Ortiz Pacheco also stated to us that Mrs. Albizu Campos called the attention of the agents of the law, indicating that they should be careful with what they intended to do, lest it turn out that the regime they represented would punish them for carrying out an illegal search.

According to the information that Mr. Ortiz Pacheco also gave us, the federal agents tried to search the residence of don Juan Caballero Flores, located in the same neighborhood "Mulas", where Mr. Albizu Campos had been living lately in the countryside, but due to another error in the name of the neighborhood, they were unable to carry out the search.

"SERENE AND ALERT!":

A large group of nationalists accompanied Mr. Albizu Campos along San José street to the Hotel Palace when yesterday's afternoon session of the trial of Dionisio Pearson ended.

Upon entering the hotel, Mr. Albizu Campos, addressing his followers, said to them: "Serene and alert!" Almost all raised their hands in a sign of greeting and some wanted to applaud, but Mr. Albizu Campos cut off the attempt, saying: "No, there are no applauses."

The crowd dispersed and shortly after Mr. Albizu Campos left by automobile for his home accompanied by his private secretary, the young Juarbe, and his professional colleague, licenciado Rafael Ortiz Pacheco.

William H. MONTALVO,
Editor.
