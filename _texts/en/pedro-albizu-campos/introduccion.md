PEDRO ALBIZU CAMPOS
Student of Harvard University
1913-1921

INTRODUCTION

By J. Benjamín Torres

The present compilation comprises articles, proclamations,
declarations, interviews, manifestos, reviews of speeches and some
correspondence of don Pedro Albizu Campos published in some of
the newspapers and magazines of Puerto Rico during the years from 1923 to
1936.

We have included his writings from 1923, the year in which he made his
formal entry into the Unión de Puerto Rico Party and began his political struggle,
until the year 1936, when he was tried in the District Court
of the United States of Puerto Rico, known as the Federal Court, for the
alleged crime of "conspiracy" and was convicted and sentenced to six
to ten years in prison.

We will see through his writings and pronouncements his political development until 1930 when he assumes the presidency of the Partido
Nacionalista de Puerto Rico and undertakes a gigantic campaign of
education and awareness. He begins to rediscover and rescue all
the values and heroic deeds of our ancestors and to fight
openly against the merciless exploitation that capitalism,
colonialism and North American imperialism maintain in our
Homeland. He becomes the Teacher of his people. He converts the tribune into
a professorship.

This gigantic and successful campaign of education and national awareness
forces imperialism to take the offensive and plot the
destruction of the nationalist movement. Indeed, from
the year 1935 a brutal repressive wave is unleashed against the Partido
Nacionalista, which culminates in the Ponce Massacre on March 21,
1937 and in the imprisonment of the nationalist leadership in the
Atlanta Penitentiary in the United States on June 7, 1937.

This anthology is part of the fruit of the research work on
the history of the Partido Nacionalista and on the life and work of don Pedro
Albizu Campos that, together with my partner Sonia E. Vergne,

we began in June 1972 as a thesis project for the Programa
de Estudios Puertorriqueños that was sponsored by the Universidad del Estado
de Nueva York in Buffalo jointly with the Instituto de Cultura
Puertorriqueña.

We have been reviewing all the newspapers and magazines of the time and
interviewing people who were and are linked to the
history of Nationalism in Puerto Rico. Much remains to be reviewed and
compiled. All this material has been scattered in different
private collections and in the different public libraries of
Puerto Rico. There is still much material not compiled or cataloged in the
different federal archives and public libraries of the United States and
in archives in some sister republics of our America. Our
work has only just begun.

CHRONOLOGY OF PEDRO ALBIZU CAMPOS

By J. Benjamín Torres

1893: June 29. Born in the city of Ponce and his parents were
doña Juliana Campos y Campos and don Alejandro Albizu y
Romero. He attended his primary education in the public schools
of Juana Diaz and Ponce.
1912: He travels to study at the Universidad de Vermont in Estados
Unidos with a scholarship from the Logia Aurora de Ponce.
1913: He moves to the Universidad de Harvard to continue his
studies on the recommendation of two professors.
1916: He receives his bachelor's degree, continues post-graduate studies and
enters the Law School of the Universidad de Harvard.
1921: He finishes his studies. He obtains diplomas in Chemical Engineering;
Letters and Philosophy, Military Sciences and Law.
He returns to Puerto Rico.
1922: He marries in Juana Diaz with Dra. Laura Meneses, of
Peruvian nationality and whom he had met at Harvard. On 17
September 1922 the Partido
Nacionalista de Puerto Rico, but Albizu Campos does not participate
in the organization of this group.
1923: Albizu Campos makes his formal entry into the Partido Unión de
Puerto Rico.
1924: March 26. His son Pedro Albizu Meneses is born in Ponce. In
the month of May he breaks with the Partido Unión and joins the
Partido Nacionalista. On May 18 he is elected first
vice president of the Partido Nacionalista.
1925: August 30. His daughter Rosa is born in Ponce. On September 6
the Partido Nacionalista commissions him to visit the countries
Latin Americans in a propaganda campaign of the nationalist ideal.
1927: June 20. He begins the pilgrimage through Latin America. He visits
firstly Santo Domingo, then Haiti, Cuba, Mexico,
Panama, Peru and Venezuela. On October 16 his daughter Laura Esperanza is born in Peru.
1930: January 4. He returns to Puerto Rico. He goes to reside in the Barrio Villa
Palmeras de Santurce. On May 11 he is elected president of the
Partido Nacionalista in the assembly held by the group
in the Ateneo and begins a gigantic campaign of education and
awareness of the people throughout the fields, neighborhoods and
public squares of Puerto Rico. His motto: "Nationalism is
the Homeland organized for the rescue of its sovereignty."
