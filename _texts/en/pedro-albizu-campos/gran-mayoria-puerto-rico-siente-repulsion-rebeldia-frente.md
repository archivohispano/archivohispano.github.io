PEDRO ALBIZU CAMPOS

"THE GREAT MAJORITY OF PUERTO RICO FEELS REPULSION AND
REBELLION AGAINST YANKEE COLONIALISM"
THUS ALBIZU CAMPOS EXPRESSES HIMSELF IN AN INTERVIEW GRANTED
TO THE DOMINICAN PRESS

Lcdo. Pedro Albizu Campos was interviewed by journalist J.B.
Lamarche upon his arrival in Santo Domingo. The text of the interview
held, which we reproduce today, appeared in one of the last
editions of "Listín Diario".-
-"Dr. Albizu Campos is a man of little accessibility, despite his
exquisite culture. Little accessible because, a journalist of pure stock, he hates
interviews, or, as he calls them, interview factories." He is
reluctant to make statements, which, many times, reproduce,
twisting and mystifying, the thinking of the declarant, and which,
in other cases, only serve as a pretext for the journalist to put in the mouth
of others everything that occurs to him. The illustrious Puerto Rican nationalist
knows the craft thoroughly. And, therefore, he evades the occasions that present themselves to express himself by this means. We, who divine
his scruples, reassured him, channeling his attention towards suggestive topics, and managed to break his vow of silence."
-What plans, Dr. Albizu Campos, do you bring to Santo Domingo?
-"My work must be mainly of organization. I try to unify
the Dominican sentiment in favor of the independence of Puerto Rico.
I have been able to note that here that sentiment is unanimous. That means that
the ground is already fertile. This is already a great advantage. I
see in this country one of the most civic nations and one that has the most character
in America. That is why I wanted to come to learn in this school."
-How do you propose to develop your propaganda work?
-"I will interview the representative elements of all sectors
of opinion in the Republic, I will get in touch with them;
I will give some conferences, if necessary; but this as a last resort
term, because I am resolved to speak as little as possible. It is not my
object to deliver beautiful speeches to reap ephemeral applause.
I desire something more intense and lasting. I want to leave here, when I
leave, a living and permanent organism that is responsible for
reproducing the palpitations of Puerto Rican nationalism outside the
Homeland, counting, of course, for this, with the cooperation of all the
elements of the colony of my country established, which I suppose
will lend themselves to it, since it is an inescapable duty."
-What policy, in its general lines, does Puerto Rican nationalism
follow?
-"Nationalism, after the death of De Diego, has

sworn requests take the voter before a magistrate, Municipal Judge
of the Colony, who administers administrative justice, that is to say that he is an instrument of the will of the Governor of the Colony, a Yankee.
In this way, when he does not object to the admission of applications
-which, as it is a technical document, can be declared null and void by
any circumstances-, he gets sick or disappears, obeying the
specific instructions of his principal."
-"As you can see, consequently, it is a mockery, a derision,
to electoral law such a procedure."
-"Upon the arrival of the 1924 elections, despite the situation created
by the Government, with the premeditated purpose that separatists should not appear
in the ballot boxes of Puerto Rico, the Nationalist Party,
waving the insignia of De Diego, went to the electoral struggle, in the
Municipalities already mentioned, with the sole purpose of avoiding the opprobrium and
shame that the flag of the Homeland did not appear in the elections of Puerto Rico, which none of the other parties dared
to wave during the election period, obeying the mandate of the
Government of Washington"
--"Recently -continues our interviewee- the situation has
been worsened. In 1924 there was among the contending servile sides
a fight for public positions, despite their understanding of stifling
the nationalist movement. Recently, the Electoral Law has been amended
to avoid the reorganization of the Parties, giving them
legal "status" that the two conjunctions called
Alliance and Republican-Socialist Coalition did not have. Thus, suffrage is violated and the country is led to the need to resort to adequate means to make
respect the will of the people, which is separatist, absolutely.
-"No one knows -ends Dr. Albizu Campos- how far a people can go
when it is harassed, exploited and reviled without measure
or quarter. And that people has to be prepared for all the
contingencies that honor imposes on it."

J.B. LAMARCHE

"TO BE HEARD, PUERTO RICO HAS TO POSE
A SERIOUS CRISIS TO THE COLONIAL ADMINISTRATION"

"IN WASHINGTON THEY HAVE DISREGARDED THEIR COMMISSIONERS,
WHO HAVE LIMITED THEMSELVES TO MAKING ANOTHER GENUFLECTION"

Says Albizu Campos from the columns of the Dominican press.

From our Dominican colleague, "La Opinión", we take the following
interview with the licentiate Albizu Campos:
"Pedro Albizu Campos... The Nationalist of Ponce... Independentist
ideals... Heroic struggle... Steel wills...
Shame... Dignity... Apostleship... When the siren of the "San
Lorenzo" greeted the Placer de los Estudios yesterday, in my mind jumped
all the words that serve to qualify the civic virtues of the
men.

Albizu Campos has not been a ballerina in politics, He hates
theatricality. He is a well-prepared intellectual. He does not like
provincial exhibitionism. He is a consecrated to the high ideals of the
nationality. Pedro Albizu Campos luminous eyes, sad smile,
tropical tawny color is a Man. (I beg the linotypist to put his
attention on the capital V).

The Vice-President of the Nationalist Party verb fiery red the Director of El Nacionalista de Ponce buffalo leather whip, Mississippian overflow was my friend: is my friend. I am
a friend of all men who love the word redemption be they
Chinese, Cantonese, Indians of Ghandi or Boricuas involuntarily subjected
to the Nordic empire. It was, therefore, easy for me to see and
converse with the granitic leader of Puerto Rican nationalism.

The interview had several episodes and different settings Time: 9 a
1 at night. Place: Parque Colón; inside a "guagua", San
Gerónimo; Parque Colón, Restaurante Fausto.
Pedro Albizu Campos, born in Ponce on September 12, 1891,
graduated from the famous University of Harvard in the courses of
Chemical Engineering, Letters and Philosophy, Military Sciences and Law,
practices his profession of Lawyer successfully. He knows what he has in
hands. His conversation is very pleasant and his answers quick and
precise.

In front of the statue of the Great Admiral, after a brief chat:
-And what should Puerto Rico do then?
-Puerto Rico has to pose a serious crisis to the
colonial administration in order to be heard in its demand. Until

now the colonist has made requests for things of little importance. In
Washington they have disregarded their commissioners and they have
resigned themselves to making another genuflection. A nation like the
North American, with enormous national and international problems,
does not have time to attend to submissive and servile men. It requires
the formation of an organization that encompasses all the people of Puerto
Rico and that breaks definitively with the regime of the colony, and
requests from the free nations the recognition of our
independence in order to achieve the reconcentration of the
North American mind on our situation."
In the "guagua", in front of two enormous ladies who spoke in tone
of B flat overacute:
- Is Puerto Rican nationalism making its way into the soul of the
people?
-"The nationalist ideology dominates the country. No one dares to
fight it. The leaders of the old and low politicking cannot
attack it openly because if they do so they would lose their influence in the
colonial government. The university youth, the new generations
that rise up are frankly, sincerely nationalist."
Albizu Campos speaks serenely; but his words are
dazzling with warm enthusiasm. We abandon the "tone" of
interview and chat about some very pleasant topics:
Vasconcelos, young intellectuals from Puerto Rico, Creole love,
Russian ideology...
The chocolate ice creams of the "Fausto" were last night as pleasant
as on Sunday. New measure of interview. It's 12 at night.
-What are, in your opinion, the pragmatic lines that should follow
Ibero-Americanism?
-"We Ibero-Americans are fully convinced of the
necessity of putting into practice the immediate organization of
Ibero-Americanism. That ideology that will restore the hegemony
world of the Iberian race, requires the constitution of an
international organization representative of all the Ibero-American countries that
works outside of the constituted governments, stimulating its
action or stopping it, according to the supreme conveniences
dictated by the purposes that are pursued. It is already time to stop the
generalizations on this subject. Once the Iberoamerican
federative organization of which we have spoken is established, it is necessary to proceed to the
realization without delay of specific ends.
"Although Europe intends to exert on Ibero-America economic and cultural influence, and would gladly enter also in
political interventions, if the circumstances so permitted, its
action does not constitute a danger for us, and rather it is a force to
counterbalance the influence of the United States of North America."
"It is necessary to study the North American penetration in the Colombian continent.
The North American interventions are military, economic,
cultural and even religious. We have to cut them all
from the root."
"Iberoamericanism will concentrate its attention on the territories most

affected by that North American imperialism, and will dislodge it from the
positions where it feels safest, as in Puerto Rico and in other
nations of the Caribbean and Central America. The case of Puerto Rico is a
synthesis of the general situation. There is one of our nationalities
more defined, of greater population density and a progress and
surprising industrial and commercial organization. The United States has
established in Puerto Rico an irresponsible regime of Government. The
Administration, the courts and the legislation, everything depends on the
supreme will of the military man who occupies the position of Supreme Chief of
insular affairs in the Department of War of the United States, and
who is responsible exclusively to the President of the North American Empire."
"The fact that in Puerto Rico, after twenty-seven years of
North American occupation a nationalist movement is consolidated
openly hostile to the colonial regime, is an indication of the effect of that
occupation. A systematic plan has been developed for dispossession
collective, absorbing the Yankee estates the best lands of the
country, the Government has given to North American Corporations the
franchises that give them exclusivity in the exploitation of all sources
of wealth, communications, and finance, and a
compulsory Public Instruction system, imposing the
instruction of all subjects in the exotic English language, with the
perverse purpose of destroying our Hispanic culture."
"Iberoamericanism should concentrate its attention on the concrete case of Puerto Rico, which summarizes the general situation. The North American policy is presented in all its nakedness before this peaceful and defenseless people ruled by the Yankee military boot."
-Do you believe that the omnipotent power of the United States
will diminish?
-"Until the year 1914, the United States was a debtor nation and
still own substantial interests within their borders,
English, French and other nationals. The European war produced the defeat of all of Europe and the Yankee financial hegemony. But that
North American power that is exercised in the roughest way to
paralyze the economic life of competing nations or to submit to
exploitation to consumer nations, is necessarily transitory. It
will liquidate it the next world war."
My ice cream is melting. I listen attentively to Albizu Campos.
-What countries will you visit?
-"I will travel through the nations of the Caribbean, Central and South America and if
there is time, I will visit the Mother Homeland Spain on this mission, where
there are currently organizations of high influence in favor of
our cause."
-Do you plan to establish any entity or organization in Santo
Domingo, that fights for the independence of Puerto Rico?
-"I expect from all Dominicans their noble cooperation to
constitute a Dominican national board pro-independence of Puerto
Rico. I know that there is not a Dominican who is not willing to cooperate in
that lofty purpose."

"Directors of the numerous Puerto Rican colony in this
hospitable country, have indicated to me the convenience of establishing
a nationalist board of Puerto Ricans pro-independence of the pained
Homeland."
"Nothing more noble, and nothing else could be expected from the patriotic sentiments of my fellow citizens."
One o'clock. Two handshakes. The rickety car of Mateito
leads me to my house. I think. I dream. There are dreams that are truly apotheosis...'

ALBIZU CAMPOS WAS SOME HOURS IN
PUERTO PRINCIPE, HAITI

THERE HE RELATED WITH THE LEADERS OF
HAITIAN NATIONALISM

"We have had the great pleasure of receiving the visit of the Lcdo. Albizu
Campos, Vice-President of the Nationalist Party of Puerto Rico and
Director of the newspaper "El Nacionalista" that is published in Ponce.
"Mr. Campos, delegate of the Puerto Rican people in the
Ibero-American Republics and in Europe, comes from Santo Domingo
where he has spent some weeks and giving lectures on
Yankee imperialism and on the need for the Latin peoples of
this hemisphere to unite to put a brake on the ferocious appetites of the
Ogre of the North.
"In all his lectures he has spoken of Haiti and in favor of the
de-occupation of its territory. Here is the thesis that he has developed
to interest Dominicans and other Hispanic-American peoples in our cause:
"The de-occupation of Haiti is a necessity for all the peoples,
Ibero-Americans, because if they do not help the Haitians liberally,
spontaneously and without conditions, the Haitian people will have to
seek with our enemies the support that we have not been able to offer them
nor give them. It is a matter of high politics to win the sympathy of Haiti by
means of eloquent manifestations and feelings of friendship and
justice that we feel for her."
"He has also spoken of the strategic situation of Haiti in the
case in which an action were undertaken against the Spanish peoples
of this hemisphere, and above all he has made the Dominicans observe
"that they have been granted the privilege of fulfilling a gesture of
transcendent chivalry helping Haiti without restriction against the
Yankee invaders and making the Dominican Republic a center of
continental propaganda in favor of Haiti."
"Since his arrival, Mr. Albizu Campos paid a visit to our
friend and collaborator Pierre Paul, who had been recommended by
our delegate in Santo Domingo, the tireless and prestigious
Enriquillo Henríquez García. After a long exchange of opinions
with our collaborator on the situation of the Latin peoples of this
hemisphere with respect to the United States of the North and on the need
of a policy of "interpenetration" between all these peoples, to

form a united and unique front and an insurmountable league for the
moral and humiliating invasions of the Yankees, the distinguished visitor
returned aboard the "Habana" on which he travels.

"A quarter of an hour later, Mr. Pierre Paul, accompanied by
our Director, to whom Campos had also been recommended,
reciprocated his visit, and as the steamer, whose departure had been
announced for 10 in the morning, was not to leave our port until
noon, Mr. Jolibois and Mr. Pierre Paul insisted that he
spend two more hours on land, which he very kindly accepted.

"Mr. Campos, in company of the two nationalist leaders,
visited Mr. Victor Cauvin, General Secretary of the Patriotic Union,
because the delegated administrator, Mr. Perceval Thoby, was on vacation;
to our colleagues of "Le Temps", of "Nouvelliste" and of
"L'Haitien", to our friend Dantés Bellegarde and to the president and
vice-president of the Haitian Chamber of Commerce, Mr. Edouard
Estéve and Simón Vieux, respectively. He also had the pleasure of
meeting Mr. I.C. Lherisson, president of the "League of Human Rights", and to Me. Pierre Hudicourt and to Mr. Rouzier, Fr.
Gaetjons and Blanchard.

"An honor "champagne" was offered to him in the "Caves de
Bordeaux" by the nationalist leaders Jolibois, Cauvin and Pierre Paul,
in the name of the Patriotic Union and the "Courrier Haitien".

"It was noon when the three leaders took the distinguished
Puerto Rican aboard and separated from him giving him a fraternal embrace.

"Mr. Campos continues for Cuba, Mexico, Central America,
South America, and Europe. He has insistently begged us to
transmit this message to the Haitian people.

"Learn Spanish if you want to defeat the Yankee ogre."

"He will not return to his Homeland, he has told us, until a year has passed, when
he has finished in America and in Europe his mission of propaganda in
favor of the independence of Puerto Rico. And he promised us to speak of
Haiti wherever he has to defend the Puerto Rican cause.

"Mr. Campos takes from Haiti a pleasant impression, prepared
to deny all the lies that the unscrupulous American has
published and continues to publish every week in Puerto Rico, where he
insists on passing off the Haitian as a semi-savage people.

"In the course of his visit to our colleague "L'Haitien", Mr.
Campos was very happy to receive as a gift from Dr.
Pressoir a copy of the History of Haiti by Dr. C. Lorsinvil.

"We renew our wishes for a good trip and
fruitful work."

EXTENSIVE DETAILS ON THE ACTIONS OF ALBIZU CAMPOS IN
THE SEVENTH INTERNATIONAL CONGRESS OF THE LATIN PRESS
From our colleague "Heraldo de Cuba" we take the following
information regarding the international congress of the Latin press
held recently in Havana:
"It was not necessary to wait long to reach the conclusion
that this VII Congress of the Latin Press has no importance whatsoever,
which, being a strong link between the spirituality and
the thought of the men who come from the same old racial
trunk, is, in short, nothing more than one of so many reasons conducive to the
promotion of tourism... The first contact with the realities that
prick in the soul of the peoples, was enough to lift the
veil behind which beautiful hopes were nourished.
"Under the pretext of not addressing political issues (who is capable,
colleagues, of defining the scope and value of politics?), it is feared,
even to make a concise and definitive statement in favor of the
freedom of the press...
Let's go to the chronicle.
A REGULATION THAT COMES OUT OF THE NIGHT TO THE MORNING
"The day before it had been agreed that none of the
previous congresses had formulated either statutes or regulations that
govern the permanent functioning of such congresses. In this virtue
it was up to put the anti-interventionist motion before the
other Ibero-American delegations, which tends to the articulation of
those statutes and regulations. The debate proceeds, and it turns out then
that M. Waleffe, a true thaumaturge, comes to argue that if there is
an earlier regulation. And even reads it! ..... One, two, three,
up to eight articles, contained in a manuscript.
"A lively discussion arises. Many, although admiring
this stupendous Mr. Waleffe who from night to morning has achieved what
six previous congresses could not achieve, maintain their doubts
about the authenticity of the document. The manuscript has the fresh ink
left by the pen of the French journalist on a sleepless night...
But how to doubt officially? This would be contrary to courtesy...
So, we already know, there is a Constitution!

A MOTION THAT SCANDALIZES THE FRENCH
BEFORE BEING READ

"Meanwhile, Pedro Albizu Campos, the tireless fighter for
the freedoms of his Homeland: Puerto Rico, had formulated a series of bases -which
we insert later- condemning the interventionist policy of the
Government of the White House in the internal affairs of various
Hispanic-American republics. The motion was already signed by
Cuban, Hispanic-American, Belgian, Italian, etc. delegates, but
before being read, upon being simply presented to the table, M. le Pétit,
delegate of the "Pétit Parisién", felt offended in the innermost of his
soul. His wrath was such that it seemed that he had been outraged in his person
himself. We have seen not many days ago to Mr. Hughes,
President of an official delegation from North America, endure a
true catilinarian of another diplomat Mr. Alvarez, of El
Salvador without losing his composure in the least. But the pétit M. le Pétit could not
bear that even something that transcended a censure towards the United States should be mentioned...
"In the blink of an eye the debate had turned
very violent over something that was not under discussion...
Albizu Campos defends the right he has to have his motion read.
Valdés de la Paz declares, emphatically, that if the Congress is not going to
address vital issues for Latinity, it is better that it be dissolved.
The French insist on discussing what is not under discussion, making
their claims in a way that seems more like a declaration of war.
The President Lugo Viña insists on maintaining order, refusing to
grant the floor on a matter that is not under discussion. Then
about ten or twelve French-speaking delegates, spectacularly,
leave the field...
"And the President adjourns the session, so that in the afternoon the
commissions are constituted.

TEXT OF THE ANTI-INTERVENTIONIST MOTION

"The text of the anti-interventionist resolutions and propositions,
signed as we said before by numerous American and
European delegates, is as follows:
"Number 1. Resolution. The Seventh Congress of the Latin Press
protests energetically against the interventions of the United States in
the affairs of the Latin American Nations, and encourages the Latin
Nations to maintain unalterable the principle of international
law that imposes on each State the duty not to intervene in
the affairs of another State.
"Number 2. Resolution. The Seventh Congress of the Latin Press
makes a solemn call to the entire world Press and especially
to the Latin Press to maintain a systematic campaign against the
occupation of the Republics of Haiti and Nicaragua by military and
naval forces of the United States.

"Number 3. – Resolution. – The Latin Press is in solidarity with the
legitimate aspirations of the Philippines and Puerto Rico to constitute
themselves into free, sovereign and independent Republics, in accordance with the
principle and the free determination of nationalities and protests
against the military occupation of these nations by forces of the United States.
"Number 4. – Proposition. – Newspapers or publications will not have the right to take part in
the Congresses of the Latin Press edited in Latin Nations that are actually branches of
newspapers or publications of non-Latin nations.
"Number 5. – Proposition. The Congress will appoint a commission
to formulate a system of exclusive world information for the Latin
Press.
"Number 6. – Resolution. – No seat will be given in the Congresses of
the Latin Press to Latin publications that support
anti-Latin propaganda within our nationalities."

ORGANIZATION OF THE COMMITTEES

"In the afternoon, with spirits calmed down, the work of the
Congress was resumed. President Lugo Viña decreed a recess so that the
four commissions were organized, which will present opinions
on the matters that correspond to them; opinions that will be
considered in plenary sessions.
"All delegates can be part of these commissions, with
the only condition that they register. The procedure is broad and
plausible. The delegates were divided into various apartments of the
Reporters Association, headquarters of this complicated Babel, and proceeded
to the election of the different board of directors of commission.
"These were constituted in the following way:
First commission. – In charge of promoting the constitution of a
Latin Information Agency.
"President: Benjamín Cohen; Vice-president: Marius Appelins and
Juan A. Dennis; Secretary: Osvaldo Valdés de la Paz; Second
Secretary: Paul Odet.
"Second commission. – University Exchange.
"President: Antonio Iraizoz; Vicepresident: Irene de Vasconcelos
and Tomé Reis; Secretary: H. Brunimon.
"Third commission – Statutes for professional journalists.
"President: M. Bóugeon; Vicepresident: Manuel Bedoya and Félix
F. Palavincini; Secretary: Juan Sánchez Azcona.
"The fourth commission could not be integrated.

INTERESTING DEBATE IN THE FIRST COMMISSION

"As soon as the first commission was constituted, its President Mr.
Cohen reads the following motion, which tends to strengthen the freedom
of the press, whose rights legitimately enshrined throughout the
world, are, however, violated at every step in almost all
latitudes.
