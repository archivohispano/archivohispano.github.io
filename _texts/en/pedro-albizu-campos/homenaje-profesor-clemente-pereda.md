Those who corral the body of the Homeland, desiring to devour it, will not be able to oppose the impetus of the supreme law. While they, sated with unconsciousness, smear their hands with the glitter of prebends, and bless the chains that darken contemporary civilization, the finger of God continues to point out the destiny of the peoples. While they candidly accept ANNEXATIONISM (statehood), repudiating the postulates of International Law, which recognizes in it a collective suicide, and stains public and private law and the name of the Nation, by virtue of which no one is entitled to be a slave, much less to violate anyone, enslaving their body and their soul; while they violate human and divine laws, occupying the abhorrent position of the highest treason. Puerto Rico will continue its triumphant march towards the glory of its INDEPENDENCE.
To carry out that miraculous march until measuring the faith of the men who suffer in the name of freedom; and penetrated by their sacrifice, until each man crystallizes in his heart the figure of the apostle. It is not licit to expect others to sacrifice themselves when we lack the will to overcome the sacrifice with weapons in hand. The time has come when every Puerto Rican is in the duty to be an apostle of his own moral burning.
Professor Clemente Pereda began the Mass of Hunger on March 24 and will rise from his torment on Holy Saturday. We harbor the sweetest hope that he will rise gloriously from his Calvary, and for this reason the Puerto Rican Nationalism, depositary of the faith of the liberators of America, announces to the Nation that on that Resurrection day, March 31 - holy in the annals of the Church and in those of the Homeland - Puerto Rico is in the sacred duty to render its homage to Professor Pereda, demonstrating thereby that the grace of God sustains those who savor the morsel of hunger, having as its emblem the cult of Liberty.
At 6 a.m. on March 31 - the day on which Professor Pereda will gird himself with the glory of having concluded his voluntary penance - all the delegations of the Nation will concentrate in the Plaza de Armas of this capital of San Juan Bautista de Puerto Rico and it is hoped that the act will take on all the majesty of a holy Resurrection.
Given in San Juan Bautista de Puerto Rico on the 28th day of March 1934, the Sixty-sixth year of the Proclamation of the Republic.

The Flag of the Race
The purposes of Puerto Rican Nationalism are:
1. The immediate restoration of the Republic of Puerto Rico.
2. The Antillean Confederation, including in it the Republic of Haiti.
3. The Ibero-American Union.
4. The hegemony in the New World of the Ibero-American nations.

For the nationalism of Puerto Rico, the need for a Flag of the Race that synthesizes the unity of the Ibero-American nations, their territorial patrimony and their civilization, is indisputable.

With the separation from the Mother Patria España, our America reconquered pre-Columbian independence, but lost its political unity despite constituting a racial unity depositary of the same civilization.

The Liberator foresaw the consequences of political division and gave the lights of his genius to restore the political unity of our nations. The greatness of his vision must be conquered by the present Ibero-American generation.

The need for this unity is not debatable. In the century and a half of independence of our nationalities, powers enemies of their existence have managed to snatch from us a precious part of the territorial racial patrimony due to the disunity of our nations, which, united, have always had and have the sufficient power to impose themselves on all imperialisms.

This resolution was published in El Mundo, on May 10, 1934, p. 2, 10 and 12 under the heading: "Correspondence on the Flag of the Race", together with other documents sent by Captain Angel Camblor of Uruguay, author of the Flag of the Race. Later, in 1971, these documents were reproduced by the Nationalist Party in: Nacionalismo enarbola la Bandera de la Raza en Izres, San Juan, Partido Nacionalista, 1971.

Before our disunion are too notorious and insolent the pretensions of the empire of the United States of North America of wanting to fix the destiny of our nationalities either by brute force or by the indirect force of economic intervention that has served to convulse the nations of the Caribbean.

The attack against our nationalities has been concentrated on the nations of the Sea of Colón, the Caribbean, but the nations of the South have also felt the sting of foreign imperialism.

It is enough to remember the case of the Malvinas Islands, a strategic position, which in enemy hands can threaten at any moment the independence of the great southern nations.

This lack of political unity has deprived us of exercising a decisive influence in the affairs of the world; influence that must be exerted by instinct of self-preservation of our own in all orders. Instead of that we contemplate the loss of territories and fabulous riches. What is more, it has even been pretended to establish the imperialism of culture in order to mold our posterity according to the conveniences of our foreign enemies.

Fortunately, the imperialism that most violently attacks us for more than a century, that is, that of the United States of North ^A,mérica, is an orphan of culture. For that reason it becomes despicable when establishing with it the first relations.

It was a yearning of the Antilleans to offer that flag of racial unity to their brothers.
