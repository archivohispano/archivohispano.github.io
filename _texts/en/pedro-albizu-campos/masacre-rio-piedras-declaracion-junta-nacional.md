In the edition of La Democracia dated October 28,
1935, and which circulated the night before, Colonel Riggs, chief
of the police in the service of the Yankee interventionist government, threatens the Puerto Rican nation
with a declaration of war. The Colonel says that there will be war, war and war!
It is necessary to note that the newspaper La Democracia is the
official organ of the Liberal Party, the governing party and currently
a fanatical defender of North American imperialism and of the administration of the President of the United States, Mr. Roosevelt.
No other newspaper has reproduced that interesting trilogy
of Colonel Riggs: war, war and war!
An act of war was the invasion of the United States Army
on July 25, 1898; an act of war constitutes the military intervention
that Puerto Rico has suffered since that fateful date; acts of war
are all the acts perpetrated by the Yankee interventionist power in
Puerto Rico during the thirty-six years of North American intervention.
The war has been waged in the name of Freedom,
of Equality, of Fraternity, of the Holy Bible, and paradoxically
also in the name of order and peace!
The nation has received the blows of war with the passive resistance
of its superior culture and its Christian civilization that have
prevented its annexation to the invading empire.
The intervening power has resorted to extreme measures of
war.
It has used all the weapons prohibited by civilization
to wage war between civilized peoples.
The goal of that war for the enemy has been none other than the
total extermination of all Puerto Ricans.

In January of 1932, as our contribution in defense of nations
unwary or confident in the good faith of the United States of
North America, we addressed the following circular to His Holiness The Pope, to the League of
Nations and to all civilized nations:

NATIONALIST PARTY OF PUERTO RICO
NATIONAL BOARD
Secretariat of Foreign Relations
Apartado 420
San Juan, Puerto Rico
Telephone 2083
San Juan, P.R.
January 1932

Sir:
In compliance with the instructions given by the President of the Nationalist Party of Puerto Rico, Don Pedro Albizu
Campos, the undersigned has the honor to inform
you of the serious matter that motivates this communication.
Under the guise of helping our people to solve public health problems, some time ago a North American medical corps of the Rockefeller Institute, an American institution that
works in close cooperation with the government of its country, the United States of North America, was established at the Presbyterian Hospital of San Juan of Puerto Rico. Apparently dedicated to curing patients victims of anemia and malaria, in reality, it has been developing a plan to exterminate our people by inoculating patients who have had the misfortune of falling under its direction, the virus of incurable diseases such as cancer.
At the date of the North American invasion, thirty-three years ago, we constituted a strong and healthy community. Wealth was well divided. There was work and abundance. The country produced almost everything it needed. There was practically no public debt. The general government and the city councils had substantial reserves in cash to face any crisis. We were a rich country in fact and in name, and joy was the common heritage of all Puerto Ricans and of the foreigners who lived with us. These facts were stated in the official reports of the first heads of the North American military occupation.
The invading power forced the displacement of Puerto Ricans
by its own North American nationals. More than fifty
thousand native landowners have disappeared and currently four
American companies own more than sixty percent of the
total wealth of the country. Barely
twenty percent of the national patrimony remains in the hands of the nationals.
The system implemented is simple: all the public burden falls
on the nationals; the Americans and their companies are practically exempt from all public contribution and enjoy the monopoly of franchises. The United States imposed its tariffs on Puerto
Rico and practically destroyed our foreign trade with other nations. This is its sixth market in the world, which averages approximately two hundred million dollars.
By virtue of this commercial monopoly, the Americans impose the conditions of purchase of our products and the conditions of sale of their products.
The mercantile monopoly is seconded by the financial monopoly.
There is practically no credit for Puerto Ricans.
The United States has mortgaged the country to its own financial interests. The military intervention destroyed agriculture.
It turned the country into a huge sugar plantation forcing it
to buy in the most expensive market in the world, that is, the North American market, which imposes on us merchandise not acceptable in any other part, under conditions and prices authorized by the monopoly it enjoys in our country. We depend on the food that the Americans sell us.
They launch on our market many products that are not fit for human consumption and that are the source of serious
diseases. There is no health regulation prohibiting the importation of food of that quality and epidemics increase.
The last head of the military occupation has just informed his government that more than sixty percent of the population is in chronic unemployment, without any means of support; that the average of salaries in the main industry, that is, the sugar industry, is one hundred and eighty dollars a year.
This means that sixty percent of the population is condemned to perish from hunger.
